# DoD Systems Engineering Guidebook Glossary

Key terms from the DoD SE Guidebook (OUSD R&E, February 2022), alphabetical, with chapter references.

**ACAT (Acquisition Category)** — the categorization (e.g., ACAT ID, IB/IC, II, III) that determines oversight and the SEP approval authority. (Ch 1)

**Allocated baseline** — the configuration baseline established at PDR, defining the functional and interface requirements allocated to system elements. (Ch 3, Ch 5)

**Alternative Systems Review (ASR)** — review confirming the preferred materiel solution(s) is feasible and requirements are understood. (Ch 3)

**Architecture Design process** — technical process that captures functional requirements and interdependencies in the system architecture, allocating functions to elements. (Ch 5)

**Critical Design Review (CDR)** — review establishing the initial product baseline; design mature enough to begin fabrication/coding. (Ch 3)

**Design consideration** — a cross-cutting specialty/compliance concern (one of 24) engineered into the design and governed via the SEP. (Ch 6)

**Development planning** — engineering analyses and technical planning performed before a program is established, to lay the technical foundation for the materiel development decision. (Ch 1)

**Digital Engineering (DE)** — connecting people, processes, data, and capabilities across an end-to-end digital enterprise, using authoritative models throughout the life cycle. Changes *how* SE is conducted, not *what* is required. (Ch 2)

**Enabling system elements** — products/processes (development, production, test, deployment, sustainment) that deliver a capability into service, keep it in service, or end its service. (Ch 1)

**ESOH (Environment, Safety, and Occupational Health)** — the safety/environmental dimension of system safety; ESOH information is documented in the SEP. (Ch 6)

**Event-driven review** — a technical review triggered by meeting documented entrance criteria, not by a calendar date. (Ch 3)

**Functional baseline** — the configuration baseline established at SFR, defining the system's functional and performance requirements. (Ch 3, Ch 5)

**Functional Configuration Audit (FCA)** — audit (with SVR) verifying the system against the functional baseline. (Ch 3)

**Human Systems Integration (HSI)** — design consideration integrating human capabilities and limitations (manpower, personnel, training, human factors, safety, habitability, survivability). (Ch 6)

**Implementation process** — technical process that matures and realizes the design of the system and system elements, generating the product baseline. (Ch 5)

**Integrated Product Team (IPT)** — a cross-functional team organizing the engineering effort; the SE Working-Level IPT (WIPT) is led by the Systems Engineer. (Ch 2)

**Interface Management** — technical management process identifying, defining, and controlling internal and external interfaces. (Ch 4)

**Issue** — a risk that has been realized, or a current problem; managed by corrective-action burn-down on the issue matrix. (Ch 4)

**Leading indicator** — a forward-looking measure signalling whether risk mitigation is on track before a threshold is breached. (Ch 4)

**Lead Systems Engineer / Systems Engineer** — prepares the SEP under the PM and leads the SE WIPT; balances cost/schedule/performance at acceptable risk. (Ch 1, Ch 2)

**Major Capability Acquisition (MCA)** — the AAF pathway whose life cycle the standard technical review sequence (ASR…PCA) is aligned to. (Ch 3)

**Manufacturing Readiness Level (MRL)** — a measure of manufacturing maturity/risk, assessed at defined points in parallel with TRLs. (Ch 6)

**Modular Open Systems Approach (MOSA)** — designing with severable modules and open interfaces/standards to the maximum extent practicable; paired with data-rights analysis. (Ch 2, Ch 6)

**Opportunity** — a potential future benefit (cost/schedule/performance); managed by pursue/reject on the opportunity matrix. (Ch 4)

**Physical Configuration Audit (PCA)** — audit confirming the product baseline matches the as-built configuration. (Ch 3)

**Preliminary Design Review (PDR)** — review establishing the allocated baseline; preliminary design meets requirements at acceptable risk. (Ch 3)

**Product baseline** — the configuration baseline initially established at CDR and verified at PCA, defining the as-designed/as-built product. (Ch 3, Ch 5)

**Production Readiness Review (PRR)** — review confirming readiness to enter production at acceptable risk. (Ch 3)

**Requirements Analysis process** — technical process decomposing top-level requirements into a complete set of functional and performance "shall" requirements. (Ch 5)

**Requirements Management** — technical management process maintaining bidirectional traceability and controlling requirement changes. (Ch 4)

**Risk** — a future event with negative consequence; scored on a likelihood × consequence matrix; handled by accept/avoid/transfer/control. (Ch 4)

**RIO (Risk, Issue, and Opportunity) management** — the Risk Management process managing three objects (risk, issue, opportunity) with three reporting matrices. (Ch 4)

**SE process model (16 processes)** — eight technical management + eight technical processes, mapped to ISO/IEC/IEEE 15288. (Ch 1, Ch 4, Ch 5)

**Stakeholder Requirements Definition process** — technical process translating operational requirements from stakeholders into top-level technical requirements. (Ch 5)

**System** — an aggregation of system elements and enabling system elements achieving a purpose or providing a capability. (Ch 1)

**System Functional Review (SFR)** — review establishing the functional baseline. (Ch 3)

**System of Systems (SoS)** — a set of independently managed/operated systems that interoperate; four types: virtual, collaborative, acknowledged, directed. (Ch 2)

**System Requirements Review (SRR)** — review confirming system requirements are baselined and traceable to capability needs. (Ch 3)

**System Security Engineering** — design consideration protecting the system and its critical program information against threats across the life cycle. (Ch 6)

**System Verification Review (SVR)** — review (with FCA) verifying the system against the functional baseline. (Ch 3)

**Systems Engineering Plan (SEP)** — the master technical plan documenting the SE approach, risks, processes, resources, metrics, products, organization, design considerations, and review timing; required for MDAPs and ACAT II/III. (Ch 1, Ch 4)

**Technical Assessment** — technical management process measuring progress, conducting reviews, and tracking TPMs/leading indicators. (Ch 4)

**Technical Data Management** — technical management process capturing and controlling technical data under a data taxonomy and data-rights strategy. (Ch 4)

**Technical Performance Measure (TPM)** — a quantitative measure tracked against a planned profile to assess design adequacy. (Ch 4)

**Technical Planning** — technical management process planning/resourcing the technical effort; outputs feed the SEP. (Ch 4)

**Transition process** — technical process formally delivering the capability and enabling system elements to end users. (Ch 5)

**Validation** — operational test confirming the system meets the operational need ("built the right thing"). (Ch 5)

**Verification** — developmental test confirming conformance to requirements ("built it right"). (Ch 3, Ch 5)

**V-diagram** — the model of technical-process flow: design down the left arm, realization up the right arm. (Ch 1, Ch 5)
