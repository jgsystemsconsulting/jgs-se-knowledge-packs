# Chapter 6: By-Phase HSI Activities — Phases D, E, and F

## Core Idea
Phase D shifts HSI from design to verification, validation, and operational certification; Phase E focuses on monitoring in-mission human performance and generating lessons learned; Phase F closes out the program through safe decommissioning and archiving HSI knowledge for future programs.

## Frameworks Introduced
- **Phase D: System Assembly, Integration and Test, Launch**: HSI focus on V&V closure for all HSI requirements, validation of end product against ConOps, completion of operations support products, and system certification for human operations.
  - When to use: Once fabrication is complete; TRR, SAR, ORR, and FRR are the milestone gates.
- **Phase E: Operations and Sustainment**: HSI focus on in-mission monitoring, lessons learned generation, and sustaining engineering improvements.
  - When to use: From launch through end of planned mission; PLAR, CERR, PFAR, and DR are the key reviews.
- **Phase F: Closeout**: HSI focus on safe decommissioning, final lessons learned archiving, and knowledge transfer to future programs.
  - When to use: From decommission decision through disposal; Disposal Readiness Review (DRR) is the gate.

## Key Concepts
- **Test Readiness Review (TRR)**: First Phase D milestone; HSI inputs include system-level test objectives, requirements, plans, and procedures with HSI content.
- **System Acceptance Review (SAR)**: Phase D acceptance gate; HSI deliverables include full V&V reporting on HSI requirements (inspection, analysis, and HITL-based closures), validated analytical models, and operations support products (training documentation, manuals, procedures).
- **Operational Readiness Review (ORR) / Flight Readiness Review (FRR)**: Pre-launch gates; HSI deliverables include objective evidence of acceptability of all human-system aspects in the operational mission context, and inputs to the Human Rating Certification Package.
- **Human Rating Certification Package**: The formal evidence package submitted to TAs to endorse end-product readiness for crewed operations; HSI provides human-system acceptability evidence.
- **"Test as You Fly" / "Test the Way We Fly"**: NASA principle cited in Phase D; HITL validation events must be conducted in conditions representative of actual operational use for the results to count toward V&V closure.
- **Post-Launch Assessment Review (PLAR)**: Phase E review evaluating readiness for full routine operations post-launch. HSI provides human-system readiness evaluation.
- **Critical Events Readiness Review (CERR)**: Phase E review evaluating readiness to execute a specific critical mission event (e.g., EVA). HSI provides domain-specific human-system readiness inputs.
- **Post-Flight Assessment Review (PFAR)**: Phase E review of how well mission objectives were met; HSI describes success of human-system mission objectives, lessons learned, and improvements needed.
- **Decommissioning Review (DR)**: Phase E/F transition; HSI may have final lessons learned and final TPM values from the operational phase.
- **Disposal Readiness Review (DRR)**: Phase F gate confirming readiness for final system disposal; HSI provides human-system inputs to decommissioning and disposal planning.
- **HSI Lessons Learned**: Documented findings from inflight testing, crew debriefs, error/incident data, human performance data, physiological indicators, and mission reports; primary Phase E/F output and input to future P/Ps.
- **TPM Final Report**: Phase F deliverable under SEE 2; records final values of all HSI Technical Performance Measures as the quantitative closure of the HSI effort.

## Mental Models
- Use Phase D to close V&V: for every HSI requirement, there must be a verification statement (from earlier phases) plus closure evidence (from Phase D test/analysis/inspection). "Pre-declared" HITL from earlier phases is assessed for closure here.
- Use Phase E monitoring data (crew time, error rates, incident data, physiological indicators) to generate lessons learned early enough in the mission to influence sustaining engineering changes that reduce remaining operational costs.
- Use Phase F as the HSI knowledge transfer point: data and lessons learned archived here directly benefit future program teams who would otherwise repeat the same discovery process at the same cost.

## Anti-patterns
- **Treating Phase D V&V as a single event**: Phase D V&V is a continuous activity across TRR → SAR → ORR/FRR. Attempting to close all HSI V&V at a single review creates schedule risk and may not satisfy the "test as you fly" standard.
- **Neglecting operational monitoring in Phase E**: The guide identifies in-mission data collection (crew time, error incidents, physiological data) as essential for generating the lessons learned that justify the HSI investment made during development.
- **Skipping Phase F knowledge archiving**: The guide notes that Phase F may overlap with early-phase activities for follow-on system designs; failing to archive lessons learned forces successor programs to repeat discovery at full cost.

## Key Takeaways
1. Phase D is about verification closure: every HSI requirement needs a traceable closure record based on inspection, analysis, or HITL test evidence meeting the "test as you fly" standard.
2. The Human Rating Certification Package is the culmination of the Phase D HSI effort for human-rated programs; it must contain objective, TA-reviewable evidence for all human-system acceptance criteria.
3. Phase E HSI monitoring shifts from design to operations: human health, performance, and error data are the key inputs for identifying what worked, what needs improvement, and what should be redesigned.
4. PFAR is the moment HSI demonstrates its return on development investment: documented links between Phase E operational success and specific HSI decisions made in Phases A–C.
5. Phase F lessons learned are the HSI legacy artifact; they must be archived in program documentation systems and communicated to successor programs.
6. Final TPM values at Phase F provide quantitative closure on every human-system performance goal established in Pre-Phase A, completing the HSI metrics lifecycle.
