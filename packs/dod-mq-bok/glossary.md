# DoD M&Q BoK Glossary

Key manufacturing-and-quality (M&Q) acquisition terms from the DoD Manufacturing and Quality Engineering Body of Knowledge (Version 3.0, July 2025), alphabetical, with chapter references. Definitions are synthesized in original wording, not reproduced from the source.

**Acquisition Strategy** — the top-level integrated plan describing a program's business, technical, and support approach; M&Q strategies (manufacturing plan, quality plan) are a subset that feeds up into it. (Ch 2, Ch 3)

**Adaptive Acquisition Framework (AAF)** — the family of acquisition pathways under DoDI 5000.02. The BoK documents the Major Capability Acquisition (MCA) path but says the practices tailor to the others (Urgent Capability, Middle Tier, etc.). (Ch 1–Ch 6)

**Advanced Product Quality Planning (APQP)** — the AS9145 five-step product/process design framework (Plan and Define; Product Design; Process Design; Product/Process Validation; Production Feedback), paired with the Production Part Approval Process (PPAP). (Ch 2, Ch 4)

**Alternative Systems Review (ASR)** — the primary MSA-phase technical review, held before Milestone A, confirming the preferred solution is affordable, producible, and operationally suitable. (Ch 2)

**Analysis of Alternatives (AoA)** — the analytical comparison of operational effectiveness, suitability, and life-cycle cost across candidate materiel solutions; products are the AoA Study Guidance, Study Plan, and Final Report. (Ch 1, Ch 2, Ch 3)

**AS6500** — the SAE Manufacturing Management Program standard; the named benchmark for a contractor's manufacturing management system and the source of the "critical manufacturing process" definition. (Ch 1–Ch 6)

**AS9100 / ISO 9001** — the Quality Management System standards used to structure and assess a contractor's QMS. (Ch 1–Ch 6)

**AS9103** — the Variation Management of Key Characteristics standard; the named best practice for identifying and controlling Key Characteristics. (Ch 2–Ch 5)

**Body of Knowledge (BoK)** — a compilation of M&Q best practices and lessons learned that supports, but does not supersede, DoD policy, guidance, or law. (Ch 1)

**Critical Characteristic (CC)** — a feature whose variation could cause loss of life or catastrophic system failure; the highest-priority class of characteristic for control. (Ch 2–Ch 6)

**Critical Design Review (CDR)** — the EMD mid-phase review that establishes the initial product baseline and transfers configuration control, freezing design changes to the formal ECP process. (Ch 4)

**Critical Technology Element (CTE)** — a technology the system depends on to meet requirements that is new, novel, or applied in a novel way posing major technological risk; the anchor of the Technology Readiness Assessment. (Ch 1, Ch 2, Ch 3)

**DCMA** — the Defense Contract Management Agency; provides contract administration, surveillance, and supply-chain monitoring, often delegated via MOA, LOD, or QALI. (Ch 1, Ch 2, Ch 4, Ch 5, Ch 6)

**Detection to Prevention (D2P)** — DCMA's surveillance strategy that shifts emphasis from end-item inspection toward process capability, risk assessment, and data-driven verification. (Ch 1, Ch 2)

**Digital thread / digital engineering** — the integrated digital data linking requirements, design, production, V&V, sustainment, and cost; carried by Technical Data Packages, PLM, and Product Manufacturing Information. (Ch 1, Ch 2, Ch 3, Ch 5, Ch 6)

**DTRAM** — the Defense Technical Risk Assessment Methodology that an Independent Technical Risk Assessment follows; focus areas include Technology, Manufacturing, Software, MOSA, Security, and RAM/Sustainment. (Ch 1–Ch 4)

**Engineering and Manufacturing Development (EMD)** — the post-Milestone-B phase that develops, integrates, and demonstrates the system through CDR, the pilot line, and the Production Readiness Review. (Ch 4)

**First Article Inspection / Test (FAI / FAT)** — verification that production-configuration articles built on the pilot line meet design requirements before low-rate production. (Ch 4)

**Full-Rate Production (FRP)** — the production-decision point at which the program commits to producing at the full planned rate. (Ch 3, Ch 4, Ch 5, Ch 6)

**Independent Logistics Assessment (ILA)** — a multi-disciplined assessment of a system's supportability and in-service health, conducted before milestones and at least every five years after IOC. (Ch 2, Ch 4, Ch 6)

**Independent Technical Risk Assessment (ITRA)** — a 10 U.S.C. §2448b-mandated independent risk judgment for MDAPs before Milestones A/B/C and the FRP decision; leverages but does not merely echo TRAs and MRAs. (Ch 1–Ch 6)

**In-Service Review (ISR)** — the major Operations and Support review that characterizes the in-service technical and operational health of a fielded system. (Ch 6)

**Integrated Product Support (IPS) Elements** — the twelve elements of the product-support package (e.g. Supply Support, Sustaining Engineering, Technical Data, Training, Facilities). (Ch 6)

**Integrated Product Team (IPT)** — the cross-functional team (Overarching, Working-level, Program-level) M&Q participates in; the contractor core design team makes most critical decisions early. (Ch 3)

**JCIDS** — the Joint Capabilities Integration and Development System; produces the ICD and CDD that define validated warfighting requirements. (Ch 1, Ch 2, Ch 3)

**Key Characteristic (KC)** — a selected feature whose variation must be controlled to meet customer requirements; the typical capability target is Cpk ≥ 1.33. (Ch 2–Ch 6)

**Life Cycle Sustainment Plan (LCSP)** — the principal sustainment-governing document after IOC, updated when the support strategy changes or at least every five years. (Ch 4, Ch 5, Ch 6)

**Low-Rate Initial Production (LRIP)** — the limited-quantity production that validates manufacturing maturity before a Full-Rate Production decision. (Ch 5)

**Manufacturing feasibility** — the determination of whether a proposed solution can be produced with available capability while meeting quality, rate, cost, and schedule; the BoK's recurring "Can it be built?" test. (Ch 1, Ch 2)

**Manufacturing Maturation Plan (MMP)** — the plan a program develops to mature a process when an MRL or capability target is missed. (Ch 1, Ch 3, Ch 4)

**Manufacturing Readiness Assessment (MRA)** — the structured assessment that measures manufacturing maturity against MRL criteria. (Ch 2–Ch 6)

**Manufacturing Readiness Level (MRL)** — the maturity yardstick (1–10) for manufacturing risk; the BoK ties specific MRL targets to phase events (e.g. MRL 4 for MSA, MRL 7/8 for EMD, MRL 9/10 for production). (Ch 1–Ch 6)

**Materiel Development Decision (MDD)** — the Milestone Decision Authority's decision authorizing entry into the acquisition life cycle and pursuit of a materiel solution. (Ch 1)

**Materiel Solution Analysis (MSA)** — the phase that analyzes candidate solutions and selects a preferred concept, ending at the Milestone A risk-reduction decision. (Ch 2)

**Mission Engineering (ME)** — a top-down approach bridging JCIDS requirements into early systems analysis, architecture, and the SE process. (Ch 1)

**National Technology and Industrial Base (NTIB)** — by statute, the national-security and dual-use R&D, production, and maintenance enterprise across the US, Canada, the UK, and Australia; must be considered in MDAP acquisition planning. (Ch 1, Ch 2, Ch 6)

**Pilot line** — a low-rate production line incorporating all key production-realism elements, used to demonstrate that processes are stable, in control, and ready for LRIP. (Ch 4, Ch 5)

**Preliminary Design Review (PDR)** — the TMRR-phase review that establishes the allocated baseline and assesses producibility, manufacturing cost, and manufacturing risk. (Ch 3)

**Process capability / control** — the discipline of confirming a process is both *capable* (meets specification, via Cp/Cpk) and *in control* (statistically predictable, via SPC and control charts); performance over time is measured by Pp/Ppk. (Ch 1–Ch 6)

**Producibility** — the composite of design and production-planning characteristics that yield the most economical fabrication, assembly, inspection, test, and acceptance; the answer to "Can it be built?". (Ch 1–Ch 6)

**Production and Deployment (P&D)** — the phase covering LRIP and Full-Rate Production through fielding to the operational user. (Ch 5)

**Production Readiness Review (PRR)** — the end-of-EMD review confirming the design and manufacturing processes are ready to transition to production. (Ch 3, Ch 4)

**Quality** — the degree to which a product's attributes and characteristics satisfy a need; managed through a Quality Management System. (Ch 1–Ch 6)

**Quality Management System (QMS)** — the formalized system (typically ISO 9001 or AS9100) documenting processes, procedures, and responsibilities for meeting quality requirements. (Ch 1–Ch 6)

**Risk, Issue, and Opportunity (RIO) management** — the DoD five-step process (Planning, Identification, Analysis, Mitigation, Monitoring) reused throughout for M&Q risk. (Ch 1, Ch 2, Ch 4, Ch 5, Ch 6)

**Statistical Process Control (SPC)** — the use of control charts (e.g. X-bar/R) and statistical tools to confirm a process is in control and to reduce variation. (Ch 2, Ch 3, Ch 4, Ch 5)

**Supply Chain Operations Reference (SCOR) Model** — the Plan-Source-Make-Deliver-Return process framework DoD materiel management uses. (Ch 6)

**Technical Data Package (TDP)** — the authoritative technical description of an item (often digital), carrying Product Manufacturing Information such as GD&T, BOM, and material specs. (Ch 1–Ch 6)

**Technology Maturation and Risk Reduction (TMRR)** — the phase that drives technology, integration, and cost risk down to commit to EMD; culminates in the PDR and EMD source selection. (Ch 3)

**Technology Readiness Assessment (TRA)** — the metrics-based evaluation of Critical Technology Element maturity, paralleling the MRA. (Ch 1–Ch 6)

**Twelve M&Q Threads (A–L)** — the constant organizing scaffold across all chapters. The DoD-unique trio is **A** (the Acquisition System), **B** (Defense Contracting), and **C** (Surveillance); the design/resource set is **D** (Technology and the Industrial Base), **E** (Design), **F** (Cost and Funding), and **G** (Materials Management); and the production set is **H** (Process Capability and Control), **I** (Quality Management), **J** (the Manufacturing Workforce), **K** (Facilities), and **L** (Manufacturing Management and Control). (Ch 1–Ch 6)

**Variability Reduction Program** — the program of Process Control Plans, FMEA, Six Sigma, and DMAIC used to shrink process variation around Key Characteristics. (Ch 3, Ch 4)

**Yield** — the measure of process uniformity: First Pass (throughput) yield, Rolling Throughput Yield, and Final Yield. (Ch 2, Ch 3)
