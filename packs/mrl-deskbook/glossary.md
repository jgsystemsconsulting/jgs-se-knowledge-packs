# MRL Deskbook Glossary

Key terms from the DoD Manufacturing Readiness Level (MRL) Deskbook (Version 2022, OSD ManTech / Joint Service–Industry MRL Working Group), alphabetical, with chapter references.

**Adaptive Acquisition Framework (AAF)** — the overarching DoD acquisition construct (governed by DoDI 5000.02) that offers several pathways for delivering capability; MRL Assessments are threaded through whichever pathway a program selects. (Ch 3)

**AS6500A (SAE)** — the Manufacturing Management Program standard used as the central contractual tasking document; its Section 4.3 requires manufacturing risks to be identified, mitigated, and tracked to closure, and it drives feasibility assessments, MRL Assessments, and Production Readiness Reviews. (Ch 6)

**AS9100D** — the IAQG Quality Management Systems standard for aviation, space, and defense organizations, used to satisfy the Quality thread across all life-cycle phases. (Ch 6)

**Critical Technology (CT)** — a technology element that is new or novel, or applied in a novel way, and that the system needs to meet its operational performance requirements inside the defined cost and schedule; the definition is drawn from the GAO TRA Guide. (Ch 2)

**Data Item Description (DID)** — the standard data-deliverable specification tailored onto a contract via the DD Form 1423 CDRL; the named manufacturing DIDs are DI-MGMT-81889B (Manufacturing Plan), DI-SESS-81974 (Assessment of Manufacturing Risk and Readiness), and DI-QCIC-81794A (Quality Assurance Program Plan). (Ch 6)

**Deep dive** — a deeper, sub-component-level examination triggered when an element turns out to be more complex than first thought; the threads are re-applied at the sub-tier and an MRL is assigned to the lowest examined unit. (Ch 4)

**Depot Activation** — the process triggered when a military depot must take on unplanned product support; it pulls in major elements of both the Sustainment Maturity Level and MRL processes. (Ch 7)

**DFARS 252.204-7012** — the clause requiring safeguarding of Covered Defense Information; when in the contract, requirements are implemented per NIST SP 800-171. (Ch 8)

**Engineering and Manufacturing Development (EMD)** — the acquisition phase, entered at Milestone B, that readies a program for production; MRL 8 is appropriate for LRIP and MRL 9 for FRP within it. (Ch 3)

**Environment (laboratory / production-relevant / production-representative / pilot line / production line)** — the escalating settings in which manufacturing capability is demonstrated, encoding progressively more production realism. (Ch 2)

**Federal System** — a facility operated by or on behalf of an executive agency; for OT cybersecurity it falls under FISMA, FIPS 200, and NIST SP 800-53. (Ch 8)

**Full-Rate Production (FRP)** — the production phase MRL 9 supports; processes must be capable, in statistical control, and affordable. (Ch 3)

**Gated Product Development** — the industry analogue to DoD milestones, where companies substitute business or engineering gates and embed the MRL criteria into their own new-product-introduction process. (Ch 7)

**Independent Technical Risk Assessment (ITRA)** — the assessment (mandated by DoDI 5000.88 for major capability acquisitions) into which an MRL Assessment feeds as a best-practice input. (Ch 3)

**Low-Rate Initial Production (LRIP)** — the production phase entered around Milestone C; MRL 8 is its nominal maturity, proven on the pilot line. (Ch 3)

**Manufacturability** — design-cycle characteristics (process capability, machine/facility flexibility, the ability to produce consistently at required cost and quality); contrasted with producibility. (Ch 2)

**Manufacturing Maturation Plan (MMP)** — the costed, scheduled mitigation plan that takes each below-target element an assessment found and attaches a path to raise it to its target MRL; every assessment should carry an MMP for the areas that fell short. (Ch 5)

**Manufacturing Readiness Assessment (MRA) / MRL Assessment** — a structured evaluation of manufacturing processes, procedures, and techniques across the object hierarchy that defines current maturity, surfaces shortfalls with their risk and cost, and grounds the plan to mature. (Ch 1, Ch 4)

**Manufacturing Readiness Level (MRL)** — a 10-level, non-linear ordinal scale describing the manufacturing maturity expected at each point in the acquisition life cycle; a risk-focusing target, not a grade. (Ch 1, Ch 2)

**Materiel Solution Analysis (MSA)** — the early acquisition phase (begun at the Materiel Development Decision) in which an MRL 4 assessment is run on each competing materiel solution. (Ch 3)

**MRL Criteria Matrix (Appendix A)** — the authoritative artifact carrying detailed criteria for each MRL, broken out by thread and sub-thread across the life cycle; descriptive, not directive — it grades maturity but does not task work. (Ch 2, Ch 6)

**MRL Users Guide** — the Microsoft Excel–based companion tool (six worksheets, an interactive matrix with six-field pop-ups, a tailorable questionnaire) that turns the criteria into a working assessment instrument. (Ch 7)

**Non-Federal System** — a contractor-owned, contractor-operated facility; for OT cybersecurity it falls under NIST SP 800-171 for protecting Controlled Unclassified Information. (Ch 8)

**Operational Technology (OT)** — programmable systems or devices that interact with the physical environment (NC machines, inspection equipment, sensors, industrial control systems); the scope of the MRL cybersecurity criteria, defined per NIST SP 800-37. (Ch 8)

**Producibility** — how readily an item can be produced while still satisfying its engineering, quality, and affordability requirements; contrasted with manufacturability. (Ch 2)

**Production, Quality, and Manufacturing (PQM) lead** — the role (working for the Program Manager under DoDI 5000.88) responsible for ensuring manufacturing, producibility, and quality risks are identified and managed across the life cycle. (Ch 1)

**Production Readiness Review (PRR)** — the systems-engineering technical review, in EMD, that an MRL Assessment feeds; assessment results are presented there to inform the Milestone C production decision. (Ch 3, Ch 6)

**Responsible organization** — the deliberately neutral term for the program or project office (industry or government) that owns cost, schedule, and performance of the acquisition activity. (Ch 1)

**Section L / Section M** — the matched RFP pair: Section L specifies what an offeror must submit and in what format; Section M specifies how that submission is scored, making manufacturing readiness operative in source selection. (Ch 6)

**Statement of Work (SOW)** — the binding contractual layer whose "shall" statements task conducting MRL Assessments, MMP-driven maturation, supplier assessments, and risk-mitigation integration. (Ch 6)

**Sustainment Maturity Level (SML)** — a sustainment-side maturity scale (from the DoD PSM Guidebook) that correlates directly to MRL criteria and maps to commercial maintenance, repair, and overhaul. (Ch 7)

**Systems Engineering Technical Review (SETR)** — the chain of technical reviews (ASR, SRR, SFR, PDR, CDR, TRR, SVR, FCA, PRR) into which the MRL Assessment feeds as a manufacturing-risk input. (Ch 3)

**Tailoring** — adapting which MRL criteria apply, and how strictly, to the specific technology, product, or program; legitimate and expected, but bounded by traceability, documentation of any dropped criterion, and reliance on trained SMEs. (Ch 7)

**Technology Maturation and Risk Reduction (TMRR)** — the acquisition phase, entered at Milestone A, whose MRL 6 target must be reached by Milestone B. (Ch 3)

**Technology Readiness Level (TRL)** — the parallel 9-level scale measuring technology/performance maturity; related to MRLs but explicitly not one-to-one, and read together as "moving manufacturing left." (Ch 2)

**Thread (and sub-thread)** — the nine categories into which manufacturing risk is decomposed (Technology and Industrial Base; Design; Cost and Funding; Materials; Process Capability and Control; Quality; Manufacturing Workforce; Facilities; Manufacturing Management), so each dimension can be traced independently from MRL 1 to MRL 10. (Ch 2)
