# DoD T&E Enterprise Guidebook Glossary

Key terms from the DoD Test & Evaluation Enterprise Guidebook (OUSD R&E, August 2022), alphabetical, with chapter references. Acronyms are spelled out on first use; statutory citations and exact thresholds are stated as the source presents them and should be re-verified against the current DoDI 5000-series before operational use.

**Acceptance Testing (AT)** — production-phase developmental test confirming each delivered unit functions; the point at which the government takes ownership and warranty coverage may begin. (Ch 5, Ch 6)

**Acquisition Decision Memorandum (ADM)** — the decision record documenting, among other things, low-rate initial production (LRIP) quantities and long-lead items at a milestone. (Ch 4, Ch 6)

**Adaptive Acquisition Framework (AAF)** — the DoD's multi-pathway acquisition structure anchored in DoDI 5000.02; the backdrop across which all T&E types apply. (Ch 1, Ch 3)

**Adversarial Cybersecurity DT (ACDT)** — developmental cyber test run with red teams and cyber defenders, used in the Defense Business Systems pathway. (Ch 8)

**Authority to Proceed (ATP)** — a gate decision marking each Business Capability Acquisition Cycle (BCAC) phase boundary; informed by test measures and reports. (Ch 8)

**Beyond Low-Rate Initial Production (BLRIP)** — the gate a program under oversight cannot pass until DOT&E reports on completed operational and live-fire test to the Secretary of Defense and Congress. (Ch 1, Ch 3)

**Business Capability Acquisition Cycle (BCAC)** — the five-phase cycle of the Defense Business Systems pathway: Capability Need Identification, Solution Analysis, Functional Requirements and Acquisition Planning, Acquisition/Testing/Deployment, and Capability Support. (Ch 8)

**Business Operations Testing (BOT)** — mission-oriented developmental test with actual users in the Defense Business Systems pathway. (Ch 8)

**Chief Developmental Tester (CDT)** — the program official, named by the PM, who drives the T&E Strategy and developmental test program. (Ch 3, Ch 4)

**Contractor Testing (CT)** — testing performed by the contractor; one of the four test communities to be integrated with government test rather than duplicated. (Ch 1, Ch 6)

**Critical Operational Issues (COIs)** — the operational questions that must be resolved by operational test; a source of DT&E evaluation criteria. (Ch 1)

**Cyber Vulnerability Identification (CVI)** — a developmental cybersecurity test event used to find vulnerabilities, applied in the Defense Business Systems pathway. (Ch 8)

**Developmental Test & Evaluation (DT&E)** — the disciplined generation of substantiated knowledge about a system's capabilities and limitations; verifies technical requirements, manages development risk, and assesses readiness for operational test. (Ch 1, Ch 3)

**DT&E Sufficiency Assessment (DTSA)** — USD(R&E) assessment (10 U.S.C. §4252 at Milestone B, §4253 at Milestone C) of the sufficiency of DT&E plans, schedule, resources, and production entrance criteria, feeding a report to Congress. (Ch 6)

**Director, Operational Test and Evaluation (DOT&E)** — the OSD principal owning operational test and evaluation (OT&E) and live-fire test and evaluation (LFT&E) policy; final approval authority for T&E strategies, operational test plans, and live-fire strategies on the oversight list. (Ch 1, Ch 3)

**Early Fielding report** — the DOT&E report issued if a program proceeds to operational use or releases procurement funds before initial operational test completes. (Ch 4, Ch 6)

**Early Operational Assessment (EOA)** — an early, largely analytical operational test event giving the OTA an early read on operational risk during technology maturation. (Ch 1, Ch 6)

**Environment, Safety, and Occupational Health (ESOH)** — the safety/environmental dimension checked at the Operational Test Readiness Review before operational test. (Ch 1)

**Failure Definition and Scoring Criteria (FDSC)** — TEMP content defining defect categories, failures affecting availability, downing events, and mission failures, added around contract award. (Ch 8)

**First Article Testing (FAT)** — production test assessing production-process and environmental-stress effects on early production units. (Ch 5, Ch 6)

**Follow-on Operational Test & Evaluation (FOT&E)** — operational test after initial operational test (IOT&E) to confirm deficiency corrections, evaluate untested aspects, and assess modifications and new increments. (Ch 1, Ch 6, Ch 8)

**Full-Rate Production (FRP)** — the production decision following a successful IOT&E; the final milestone TEMP supports it. (Ch 6)

**Full-Up System-Level (FUSL) testing** — the live-fire stage where a combat-equipped, production-representative system is tested as a whole; required under 10 U.S.C. §4172, subject only to a bounded FUSL waiver. (Ch 1, Ch 6)

**FUSL waiver** — the statutory relief from full-up/end-to-end live-fire testing (never from LFT&E itself), requiring a two-part congressional package: a certification of need plus an alternative LFT&E plan grounded in actual testing. (Ch 1, Ch 4, Ch 6)

**Initial Operational Test & Evaluation (IOT&E)** — the statutory operational test (10 U.S.C. §4171) using production or production-representative articles under realistic combat conditions to judge operational effectiveness, suitability, survivability, and lethality; required for full-rate production. (Ch 1, Ch 6, Ch 8)

**Integrated Decision Support Key (IDSK)** — required T&E Strategy content (DoDI 5000.89) correlating acquisition/technical/program decisions to the contractor, developmental, live-fire, operational, and modeling-and-simulation (M&S) data that informs them. (Ch 1, Ch 2, Ch 6)

**Independent Technical Risk Assessment (ITRA)** — an independent technical-risk assessment required on Major Defense Acquisition Programs before Milestones A and B and production entry; its report subsumes the Technology Readiness Assessment. (Ch 6)

**Joint Urgent / Emergent Operational Need (JUON / JEON / UON)** — categories of validated urgent need that trigger and scope the Urgent Capability Acquisition pathway. (Ch 4)

**Level-of-Test Determination** — the OTA judgment (Level 2 or below vs. Level 3) of operational-test rigor for a limited deployment, submitted to DOT&E for approval. (Ch 8)

**Live Fire Test & Evaluation (LFT&E)** — testing that characterizes survivability (avoiding/withstanding/recovering from threats) and lethality (defeating expected targets) against likely combat threats; non-waivable. (Ch 1, Ch 6)

**Major Capability Acquisition (MCA)** — the deliberate, full-rigor five-phase pathway (Materiel Solution Analysis, TMRR, EMD, Production & Deployment, Operations & Support) for the DoD's largest programs, governed by DoDI 5000.85. (Ch 6)

**Major Range and Test Facility Base (MRTFB)** — the designated core set of DoD T&E infrastructure overseen by the Test Resource Management Center (TRMC). (Ch 3)

**Middle Tier of Acquisition (MTA)** — the pathway (DoDI 5000.80) for capabilities mature enough to prototype or field within five years, split into Rapid Prototyping and Rapid Fielding. (Ch 5)

**Milestone Decision Authority (MDA)** — the official who approves phase entries and milestone decisions; on the oversight list, also relevant to TEMP/DT&E-plan approval. (Ch 4, Ch 6)

**Minimum Viable Capability Release (MVCR)** — in the Software Acquisition pathway, the first feature set fit for operational fielding, due within one year of funds obligation; triggers an IOT&E. (Ch 7)

**Minimum Viable Product (MVP)** — an early software version fielded to users for feedback, explicitly not for operational use; a developmental evaluation. (Ch 7)

**Operational Assessment (OA)** — an operational test event with more realism than an EOA, often a risk-reduction event ahead of IOT&E. (Ch 1, Ch 6)

**Operational Demonstration (ops demo)** — an operational event characterizing technical maturity, interoperability, and risk in a threat-realistic environment; the closing event of Rapid Prototyping. (Ch 1, Ch 5)

**Operational Test & Evaluation (OT&E)** — evaluation of how units equipped with the system perform under realistic operational conditions and threats, judging operational effectiveness, suitability, and survivability. (Ch 1, Ch 3)

**Operational Test Agency (OTA)** — the independent Service or defense organization (ATEC, OPTEVFOR, MCOTEA, AFOTEC, JITC) that conducts operational test and holds the authoritative record of data certified for operational evaluation. (Ch 3, Ch 4)

**Operational Test Readiness Review (OTRR)** — the gate before an operational event, verifying reliability, maintainability, supportability, and hazard/ESOH/software-safety manageability. (Ch 1)

**Production Qualification Testing (PQT)** — post-Milestone-C confirmation that manufacturing processes, equipment, and procedures are effective; repeated on design or source change. (Ch 5, Ch 6)

**Program Manager (PM)** — the official who owns and resources the T&E program, charters the T&E WIPT, and authors the T&E Strategy/TEMP. (Ch 2, Ch 3)

**Shared body of evidence / shared data repository** — a common, accessible test-data store the PM stands up so contractor, developmental, live-fire, and operational evaluators can reuse data captured once rather than re-test. (Ch 5, Ch 6, Ch 7, Ch 8)

**Software Acquisition pathway** — the two-phase pathway (DoDI 5000.87) delivering software capability continuously; first effective capability within one year of funding, then annual-or-faster releases. (Ch 7)

**T&E Oversight List** — the joint DOT&E + USD(R&E) list (Controlled Unclassified Information) of programs under OSD T&E oversight; membership flips approval authority for the T&E Strategy and test plans to OSD. (Ch 3)

**T&E Strategy** — the principal, pathway-agnostic T&E planning deliverable and stakeholder agreement on tasks, roles, resources, and responsibilities; appears as a TEMP, SAMP, or "test strategy" depending on pathway. (Ch 1, Ch 2)

**T&E Working-level Integrated Product Team (T&E WIPT)** — the cross-organization team the PM charters to coordinate T&E planning, define data requirements and resources, and push T&E content into RFPs and contracts. (Ch 2, Ch 3, Ch 6)

**Test and Evaluation Master Plan (TEMP)** — a common form of the T&E Strategy; the milestone-keyed master plan in the Major Capability Acquisition and Defense Business Systems pathways. (Ch 2, Ch 6, Ch 8)

**Test Resource Management Center (TRMC)** — the USD(R&E) advisor and infrastructure authority overseeing the MRTFB and programs (CTEIP, JMETC, NCRC, TENA, T&E/S&T) that keep test capability ahead of weapons technology. (Ch 3)

**USD(R&E) (Under Secretary of Defense for Research and Engineering)** — the OSD principal owning developmental test and the test infrastructure; approves the DT&E plan in the TEMP and prepares Milestone B/C DT&E sufficiency assessments for MDAPs. (Ch 3)

**Validated Online Life Cycle Threat (VOLT)** — the authoritative, system-specific threat assessment testers use to define the threat environment and build scenarios; required for MDAPs and oversight-list programs. (Ch 2, Ch 4, Ch 6)

**Verification, Validation, and Accreditation (VV&A)** — the process applied to M&S, automated test capabilities, and virtual/pre-production environments whose data will feed operational evaluations. (Ch 2, Ch 7)
