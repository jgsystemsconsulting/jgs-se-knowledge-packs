# Chapter 11: Codes of Conduct, Penalties, and Enforcement (Chapters X-XII, Articles 95-101)

## Core Idea
Chapter X establishes voluntary codes of conduct for AI systems that are not high-risk, extending good-practice requirements beyond the mandatory scope. Chapter XI sets out the Commission's delegated-act and committee-procedure powers. Chapter XII lays down the penalty structure: three fine tiers keyed to the severity of violation, with the highest tier reserved for prohibited practices.

## Frameworks Introduced
- **Article 95 — Codes of Conduct for Non-High-Risk AI**: The AI Office and Member States encourage voluntary codes covering some or all Chapter III Section 2 requirements for AI systems not classified as high-risk. Codes may also address: EU ethical AI guidelines, environmental sustainability (energy efficiency, efficient programming), AI literacy, inclusive and diverse design, and impact on vulnerable persons/groups. Providers, deployers, and their representative organisations may draw up codes covering one or more AI systems.
  - When to use: When an operator wants to demonstrate responsible AI practices for non-high-risk systems, or when seeking to shape the voluntary compliance landscape.
- **Article 96 — Commission Guidelines**: The Commission must issue implementation guidelines covering: Art 8-15 requirements; prohibited practices (Art 5); substantial modification; transparency obligations (Art 50); relationship with Union harmonisation legislation and other Union law; and the AI system definition (Art 3(1)). Guidelines must consider SME needs, local public authorities, and most-affected sectors.
  - When to use: As the primary soft-law compliance reference once published; guidelines are not legally binding but are highly persuasive in enforcement and conformity assessment.
- **Article 99 — Three-Tier Penalty Structure**:
  - **Tier 1 (highest)**: Violations of Art 5 (prohibited practices) — up to EUR 35 million or 7% of global annual turnover, whichever is higher.
  - **Tier 2 (mid)**: Violations of operator obligations (Art 16, 22, 23, 24, 26) and notified body requirements, and transparency obligations (Art 50) — up to EUR 15 million or 3% of global annual turnover, whichever is higher.
  - **Tier 3 (lowest)**: Supply of incorrect, incomplete, or misleading information to notified bodies or competent authorities — up to EUR 7.5 million or 1% of global annual turnover, whichever is higher. For SMEs, the lower of the percentage or the absolute amount applies.
  - When to use: When assessing regulatory risk exposure, designing compliance priorities, or advising on enforcement risk.
- **Article 101 — GPAI Provider Fines (Commission-imposed)**: For intentional or negligent violations of GPAI obligations, failure to comply with information requests (Art 91), measures (Art 93), or evaluation access (Art 92) — up to 3% of global annual turnover or EUR 15 million, whichever is higher. The Court of Justice has unlimited jurisdiction to review and adjust these fines.
  - When to use: When assessing GPAI-specific enforcement exposure; Commission (not national authorities) imposes these fines.

## Key Concepts
- **Proportionality factors** (Art 99(7)) — when setting fine amounts, authorities must consider: nature, gravity, and duration of infringement; number of affected persons; damage level; previous fines on the same operator; size and market share; financial benefits gained from infringement; degree of cooperation; degree of technical/organisational measures taken; how the infringement came to light; intentional or negligent character; actions taken to mitigate harm.
- **SME fine cap** (Art 99(6)) — for SMEs including start-ups, each fine is capped at the lower of the percentage-based or absolute-amount thresholds, providing a meaningful reduction from the standard maxima.
- **Public authority fine ceiling** (Art 100) — Union institutions, bodies, offices, and agencies are subject to fines imposed by the European Data Protection Supervisor, with lower maxima: EUR 1.5 million for prohibited practices; EUR 750,000 for other violations.
- **Annual fine reporting** (Art 99(11)) — Member States must report annually to the Commission on administrative fines issued and related litigation.
- **Whistleblower protection** (Art 87) — Directive 2019/1937 applies to persons reporting infringements of the Act; standard Union whistleblower protection framework applies.

## Mental Models
- **"Fine tiers track risk tiers"**: Prohibited practices (highest societal harm) = highest fines. Operator obligations (significant but mitigable harm) = mid fines. Information supply errors (procedural) = lowest fines. This mirrors the regulatory priority structure of the Act.
- **"GPAI fines are Commission-only"**: National authorities cannot fine GPAI model providers for Chapter V violations; only the Commission (acting through the AI Office) can. This reflects exclusive Union enforcement jurisdiction over GPAI.

## Anti-patterns
- Treating voluntary codes of conduct as having the same legal standing as mandatory requirements: Art 95 codes are voluntary; compliance does not satisfy mandatory obligations under Chapter III Section 2. However, they may be relevant as evidence of good-faith responsible AI practices.
- Underestimating SME provisions: the Act has multiple SME-specific alleviations including simplified documentation, lower fine caps, and sandbox access. SME status is a compliance planning variable, not just a self-description.
- Assuming penalty percentages apply to local revenue: fines are based on total worldwide annual turnover of the undertaking in the preceding financial year, not revenue from EU activities alone.

## Key Takeaways
1. Three-tier fine structure: 7%/EUR 35M (prohibited), 3%/EUR 15M (operator/transparency obligations), 1%/EUR 7.5M (misleading information).
2. For SMEs, the lower of the percentage and absolute amount applies — a genuine cap, not merely a guideline.
3. GPAI provider fines (Art 101) are imposed by the Commission, not national authorities; up to 3% of global turnover.
4. Codes of conduct (Art 95) extend responsible AI practices to non-high-risk systems; they are voluntary but may be approved by the Commission as implementing acts.
5. Commission implementation guidelines (Art 96) are required to cover all key practical questions; these will be the primary compliance reference for operators alongside harmonised standards.
6. Whistleblower protection applies to persons reporting AI Act infringements.

## Connects To
- **Chapter II (Art 5)**: Violations incur Tier 1 fines — the highest tier.
- **Chapter III Section 3 (Art 16, 22-24, 26)**: Operator obligation violations incur Tier 2 fines.
- **Chapter V (Art 53-55)**: GPAI violations subject to Art 101 Commission fines.
- **Chapter VII (Art 64)**: AI Office administers Commission fines for GPAI violations.
