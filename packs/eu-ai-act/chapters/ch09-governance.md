# Chapter 9: Governance — AI Office, AI Board, National Authorities (Chapter VII, Articles 64-70)

## Core Idea
Chapter VII creates a three-tier governance architecture: the AI Office at Union level (within the Commission) with exclusive competence over GPAI models; the European Artificial Intelligence Board as the Member State coordination body; and national competent authorities (notifying authority + market surveillance authority) in each Member State. A scientific panel of independent experts and an advisory forum provide technical input and stakeholder voice.

## Frameworks Introduced
- **Article 64 — AI Office**: The Commission's internal function for implementing, monitoring, and supervising AI systems and GPAI models and AI governance across the Union. References to the AI Office are legally references to the Commission. The AI Office develops Union expertise and capability in AI.
  - When to use: When identifying who has primary enforcement jurisdiction over GPAI models (exclusively) and coordination responsibility across the Act.
- **Article 65-66 — European Artificial Intelligence Board (the Board)**: One representative per Member State (European Data Protection Supervisor as observer; AI Office attends without voting). The Board advises and assists the Commission and Member States for consistent and effective application of the Act. Key tasks: coordination, best-practice sharing, harmonisation of administrative practices, advice on GPAI enforcement, recommendations on Annex III amendments, opinions on delegated acts, AI literacy promotion.
  - When to use: When understanding how cross-border consistency of implementation will be achieved and who issues guidance affecting national authorities.
- **Article 67 — Advisory Forum**: Balanced stakeholder body (industry, SMEs, start-ups, civil society, academia) appointed by the Commission for two-year renewable terms. Permanent members include FRA, ENISA, CEN, CENELEC, ETSI. Provides technical expertise and advises the Board and Commission. Publishes annual activity reports.
  - When to use: When seeking to participate in standards-setting and code of practice development, or understanding how stakeholder input reaches the Board.
- **Article 68 — Scientific Panel of Independent Experts**: Commission-established expert body supporting AI Office enforcement, particularly for GPAI models. Core tasks: alert AI Office to systemic risks (Art 90 qualified alerts), contribute to evaluation tools and benchmarks, advise on systemic-risk GPAI classification, and support market surveillance authorities.
  - When to use: When understanding how GPAI systemic-risk assessments are technically grounded and how scientific alerts are generated.
- **Article 70 — National Competent Authorities**: Each Member State must establish or designate at least one notifying authority and one market surveillance authority. They operate independently and impartially. Member States must report on resource adequacy to the Commission every two years from August 2025. For Union institutions and agencies, the European Data Protection Supervisor acts as competent authority.
  - When to use: When identifying which national body an operator must contact, register with, or cooperate with.

## Key Concepts
- **Notifying authority** (Art 3(19)) — the national authority responsible for assessing, designating, and monitoring conformity assessment bodies (notified bodies).
- **Market surveillance authority** (Art 3(26)) — the national authority conducting market surveillance activities under Regulation (EU) 2019/1020; for AI, responsible for post-market oversight and enforcement.
- **Single point of contact** — each Member State designates one market surveillance authority as the single point of contact for the Act; the Commission publishes the list.
- **Scientific panel qualified alert** (Art 90) — a formal alert from the scientific panel to the AI Office where it suspects a GPAI model poses concrete identifiable risk at Union level or meets the systemic-risk threshold. Triggers Commission investigation powers under Art 91-94.
- **AI Office exclusive GPAI enforcement competence** (Art 88) — the Commission (via the AI Office) has exclusive jurisdiction to supervise and enforce Chapter V (GPAI obligations); market surveillance authorities may request Commission action but cannot themselves enforce GPAI obligations.
- **ADCO sub-group** (Art 65(6)) — the standing sub-group of the Board for market surveillance cooperation, designated to serve as the administrative cooperation group (ADCO) under Regulation (EU) 2019/1020 for this Act.

## Mental Models
- **"Union = AI Office; Member States = national competent authorities; bridge = the Board"**: The Act creates a dual-track enforcement system. AI Office enforces GPAI (Chapter V); national authorities enforce everything else. The Board and its sub-groups coordinate across tracks.
- **"Scientific panel is the early-warning system"**: Independent experts provide qualified alerts when they suspect systemic GPAI risks; this triggers Commission investigation without waiting for a formal complaint. It is a proactive rather than reactive mechanism.

## Anti-patterns
- Expecting a single Union AI regulator: there is no central AI Agency; the AI Office is a function within the Commission, not an independent agency. National competent authorities retain enforcement primacy for non-GPAI AI systems.
- Underestimating resource adequacy reporting: Member States must report every two years on financial and human resources; inadequate resources are subject to Board scrutiny and could trigger Commission action.
- Confusing advisory forum with decision-making authority: the advisory forum advises the Board and Commission; it has no formal decision-making power, though it can issue opinions and recommendations.

## Key Takeaways
1. Three-tier governance: AI Office (Union, GPAI), national competent authorities (domestic, non-GPAI), Board (coordination and advice).
2. The AI Office has exclusive enforcement jurisdiction over GPAI model providers (Chapter V); national authorities enforce all other chapters.
3. Each Member State must designate at least two authorities (notifying + market surveillance) and one single point of contact by 2 August 2025.
4. The scientific panel of independent experts is the technical backbone of GPAI risk assessment; it issues qualified alerts that can trigger Commission enforcement.
5. Advisory forum balances commercial and non-commercial stakeholders; FRA and ENISA are permanent members, providing rights and cybersecurity expertise.
6. Member State resource adequacy is reported every two years; the Commission facilitates experience exchange.

## Connects To
- **Chapter V (Art 51-58)**: AI Office enforces all GPAI provisions.
- **Chapter VIII (Art 71)**: AI Office is controller of the EU database.
- **Chapter IX (Art 72-94)**: Market surveillance authorities and AI Office exercise post-market monitoring and enforcement.
- **Chapter X (Art 95-96)**: AI Office facilitates codes of conduct and issues guidelines.
