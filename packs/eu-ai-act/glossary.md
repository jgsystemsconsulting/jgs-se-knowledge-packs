# EU AI Act Glossary

**Source**: Regulation (EU) 2024/1689, primarily Article 3 (Definitions) and operative articles. Definitions marked with an article reference are statutory; others are synthesised from operative provisions.

---

**Advisory Forum** — Stakeholder body appointed by the Commission (balanced: industry, SMEs, start-ups, civil society, academia) advising the AI Board and Commission. Permanent members include FRA, ENISA, CEN, CENELEC, ETSI. Art 67.

**AI literacy** — Skills, knowledge, and understanding allowing providers, deployers, and affected persons to make informed AI deployment decisions and gain awareness of opportunities, risks, and harm. Art 3(56).

**AI Office** — The Commission's function for implementing, monitoring, and supervising AI systems and GPAI models across the Union. References to it are legally references to the Commission. Art 64.

**AI regulatory sandbox** — A controlled framework set up by a competent authority offering providers the possibility to develop, train, validate, and test innovative AI systems, including in real-world conditions, under regulatory supervision for a limited time. Art 3(55).

**AI system** — A machine-based system designed to operate with varying levels of autonomy and that may exhibit adaptiveness after deployment, which, for explicit or implicit objectives, infers from input how to generate outputs (predictions, content, recommendations, decisions) that can influence physical or virtual environments. Art 3(1).

**Authorised representative** — A Union-established natural or legal person who has received and accepted a written mandate from a provider to perform and carry out on the provider's behalf the obligations and procedures under the Act. Art 3(5).

**Automation bias** — The tendency of persons to over-rely on AI outputs, particularly for recommendations supporting human decisions. Art 14(4)(b).

**Biometric categorisation system** — An AI system for the purpose of assigning natural persons to specific categories on the basis of their biometric data (unless ancillary to another commercial service and strictly necessary for technical reasons). Art 3(40).

**Biometric data** — Personal data resulting from specific technical processing relating to the physical, physiological, or behavioural characteristics of a natural person (e.g. facial images, fingerprints). Art 3(34).

**Biometric identification** — Automated recognition of physical, physiological, behavioural, or psychological features for the purpose of establishing identity by comparing biometric data of an individual to a stored database. Art 3(35).

**Biometric verification** — Automated, one-to-one verification (authentication) of a natural person's identity by comparing their biometric data to previously provided biometric data. Art 3(36).

**CE marking** — A marking by which a provider indicates that an AI system conforms with Chapter III Section 2 requirements and applicable Union harmonisation legislation. Art 3(24).

**Certificate of conformity** — Document issued by a notified body after successful third-party conformity assessment; valid for up to 5 years. Art 44.

**Common specification** — A set of technical specifications adopted by the Commission by implementing act to provide means to comply with certain Act requirements, used where harmonised standards do not exist or are insufficient. Art 3(28).

**Conformity assessment** — The process of demonstrating whether Chapter III Section 2 requirements for a high-risk AI system have been fulfilled. Art 3(20).

**Conformity assessment body** — A body performing third-party conformity assessment activities including testing, certification, and inspection. Art 3(21).

**Critical infrastructure** — Critical infrastructure as defined in Directive (EU) 2022/2557 Art 2(4). Art 3(62).

**Data governance and management practices** — Systematic processes covering design choices, data collection and origin, preparation operations (annotation, cleaning, enrichment), assumptions, bias examination, bias mitigation, and gap identification. Art 10(2).

**Deep fake** — AI-generated or manipulated image, audio, or video content resembling existing persons, objects, places, entities, or events that would falsely appear authentic or truthful. Art 3(60).

**Deployer** — A natural or legal person, public authority, agency, or other body using an AI system under its authority, except when used in the course of a personal non-professional activity. Art 3(4).

**Distributor** — A natural or legal person in the supply chain, other than the provider or importer, that makes an AI system available on the Union market. Art 3(7).

**Downstream provider** — A provider of an AI system (including a general-purpose AI system) that integrates a GPAI model, whether self-developed or obtained from another entity under contractual relations. Art 3(68).

**Emotion recognition system** — An AI system for identifying or inferring emotions or intentions of natural persons on the basis of their biometric data. Art 3(39).

**EU Declaration of Conformity (DoC)** — A document drawn up by the provider stating that the high-risk AI system complies with the Act (and applicable harmonisation legislation). Contents specified in Annex V. Art 47.

**Floating-point operation (FLOP)** — Any mathematical operation or assignment involving floating-point numbers; cumulative training compute measured in FLOPs is the quantitative proxy for GPAI systemic risk. Art 3(67).

**Fundamental Rights Impact Assessment (FRIA)** — Assessment required before deploying Annex III high-risk AI (except area 2) by public authorities and public-service private entities, covering affected persons, risks, human oversight, and risk response. Art 27.

**General-purpose AI (GPAI) model** — An AI model trained with large amounts of data using self-supervision at scale, displaying significant generality and capable of performing a wide range of distinct tasks, integrable into a variety of downstream systems; excludes R&D/prototyping-only models. Art 3(63).

**General-purpose AI system** — An AI system based on a GPAI model with capability to serve a variety of purposes, for direct use or integration in other AI systems. Art 3(66).

**Harmonised standard** — A standard adopted by CEN, CENELEC, or ETSI at the Commission's request, published in the Official Journal; compliance creates a presumption of conformity with corresponding Act requirements. Art 3(27).

**High-impact capabilities** — Capabilities matching or exceeding the capabilities recorded in the most advanced GPAI models; the qualitative criterion for systemic-risk classification. Art 3(64).

**Importer** — A natural or legal person located or established in the Union that places on the market an AI system bearing the name or trademark of a person established in a third country. Art 3(6).

**Informed consent** — For real-world testing: a subject's freely given, specific, unambiguous, and voluntary expression of willingness to participate after being informed of all relevant aspects. Art 3(59).

**Intended purpose** — The use for which an AI system is intended by the provider, including specific context and conditions of use, as specified in documentation, instructions for use, promotional and sales materials, and technical documentation. Art 3(12).

**Instructions for use** — Information provided by the provider to inform the deployer of the AI system's intended purpose and proper use. Art 3(15).

**Making available on the market** — Supply of an AI system or GPAI model for distribution or use on the Union market in the course of a commercial activity, whether paid or free. Art 3(10).

**Market surveillance authority** — National authority carrying out activities and measures pursuant to Regulation (EU) 2019/1020. Art 3(26).

**National competent authority** — A notifying authority or market surveillance authority. For Union institutions, the European Data Protection Supervisor acts as competent authority. Art 3(48).

**Notified body** — A conformity assessment body designated and notified by a Member State notifying authority and included in the EU notification database; has exclusive authority to issue AI certificates of conformity. Art 3(22).

**Notifying authority** — The national authority responsible for assessing, designating, and monitoring conformity assessment bodies. Art 3(19).

**Operator** — Umbrella term: provider, product manufacturer, deployer, authorised representative, importer, or distributor. Art 3(8).

**Performance of an AI system** — The ability of an AI system to achieve its intended purpose. Art 3(18).

**Placing on the market** — The first making available of an AI system or GPAI model on the Union market. Art 3(9).

**Post-market monitoring (PMM) system** — All activities carried out by providers to collect and review experience from AI system use, to identify any need for corrective or preventive actions. Art 3(25).

**Post-remote biometric identification system** — A remote biometric identification system other than a real-time system; capture, comparison, and identification do not occur without significant delay. Art 3(43).

**Provider** — A natural or legal person, public authority, agency, or other body that develops or has developed an AI system or GPAI model and places it on the market or puts it into service under their own name or trademark. Art 3(3).

**Publicly accessible space** — Any publicly or privately owned physical place accessible to an undetermined number of natural persons, regardless of access conditions or capacity restrictions. Art 3(44).

**Putting into service** — The supply of an AI system for first use directly to the deployer or for own use in the Union for its intended purpose. Art 3(11).

**Quality Management System (QMS)** — A documented system with 13 mandatory components (regulatory strategy, design control, development procedures, examination/validation, technical specs, data management, RMS, PMM, incident reporting, authority communications, record-keeping, resource management, accountability framework) required of all providers of high-risk AI. Art 17.

**Reasonably foreseeable misuse** — Use of an AI system not in accordance with its intended purpose but resulting from reasonably foreseeable human behaviour or interaction with other systems. Art 3(13).

**Real-time remote biometric identification (RBIS) system** — A remote biometric identification system where capture, comparison, and identification all occur without significant delay. Art 3(42).

**Remote biometric identification system** — An AI system for identifying natural persons without their active involvement, typically at a distance, by comparing biometric data with a reference database. Art 3(41).

**Risk** — The combination of the probability of an occurrence of harm and the severity of that harm. Art 3(2).

**Risk Management System (RMS)** — A continuous iterative lifecycle process for high-risk AI systems, comprising risk identification/analysis, estimation/evaluation, post-market data evaluation, and adoption of mitigation measures. Art 9.

**Safety component** — A component of a product or AI system fulfilling a safety function, whose failure endangers health, safety, or property. Art 3(14).

**Scientific panel** — Commission-established independent expert panel supporting AI Office enforcement, particularly for GPAI systemic risk alerts and evaluation methodology. Art 68.

**Sensitive operational data** — Operational data related to activities of prevention, detection, investigation, or prosecution of criminal offences whose disclosure could jeopardise criminal proceedings. Art 3(38).

**Serious incident** — An incident or malfunction directly or indirectly leading to death, serious health harm, serious and irreversible disruption of critical infrastructure, infringement of fundamental rights obligations, or serious harm to property or environment. Art 3(49).

**Social scoring** — Evaluation or classification of natural persons over a period of time based on social behaviour or known, inferred, or predicted personal characteristics leading to detrimental or disproportionate treatment. Prohibited under Art 5(1)(c).

**Substantial modification** — A post-placement change to an AI system that was not foreseen in the initial conformity assessment AND affects compliance with Section 2 requirements or results in a modification of intended purpose. Triggers new provider liability. Art 3(23).

**Systemic risk** — Risk specific to high-impact capabilities of GPAI models, having significant Union-market impact due to reach or negative effects on public health, safety, security, fundamental rights, or society, propagable at scale across the value chain. Art 3(65).

**Testing data** — Data used for independent evaluation of an AI system to confirm expected performance before placement. Art 3(32).

**Testing in real-world conditions** — Temporary testing of an AI system for its intended purpose in real-world conditions outside a laboratory/simulated environment, to gather reliable data and verify conformity; does not constitute placing on the market. Art 3(57).

**Training data** — Data used for training an AI system through fitting its learnable parameters. Art 3(29).

**Validation data** — Data used for evaluating a trained AI system and tuning non-learnable parameters and the learning process, to prevent underfitting or overfitting. Art 3(30).

**Widespread infringement** — An act or omission contrary to Union law protecting individual interests that has harmed (or is likely to harm) collective interests in at least two Member States, or occurs concurrently in at least three Member States with common features. Art 3(61).

**Withdrawal** — Any measure aimed at preventing an AI system in the supply chain from being made available on the market. Art 3(17).
