# Chapter 4: GOVERN Function

## Core Idea
GOVERN is the cross-cutting, always-on function that establishes the organisational culture, policies, accountability structures, and workforce practices required for AI risk management; it infuses and enables the MAP, MEASURE, and MANAGE functions throughout the AI system lifecycle.

## Frameworks Introduced
- **GOVERN Function**: Six category groups (GV.1–GV.6) covering policies and procedures, accountability structures, workforce diversity, risk culture, external engagement, and third-party/supply-chain governance.
  - When to use: Establishing or auditing an organisation's AI risk management programme before or alongside system-specific risk activities.

## Key Concepts
- **GOVERN 1 (Policies, processes, procedures)**: Policies and processes for mapping, measuring, and managing AI risks are in place, transparent, and implemented effectively; includes legal/regulatory compliance, risk tolerance definition, AI system inventory, and decommissioning procedures.
- **GOVERN 1.1**: Legal and regulatory requirements involving AI are understood, managed, and documented.
- **GOVERN 1.6**: Mechanisms are in place to inventory AI systems, resourced according to organisational risk priorities.
- **GOVERN 1.7**: Processes for decommissioning and phasing out AI systems safely, without increasing risk or decreasing trustworthiness.
- **GOVERN 2 (Accountability structures)**: Roles, responsibilities, lines of communication, training, and executive accountability for AI risk management are documented and implemented.
- **GOVERN 2.3**: Executive leadership takes responsibility for decisions about risks associated with AI system development and deployment.
- **GOVERN 3 (Workforce diversity, equity, inclusion, accessibility)**: Processes prioritise diverse teams and define human-AI configuration roles and oversight responsibilities (GOVERN 3.1 diversity in decision-making; GOVERN 3.2 human-AI configuration policies).
- **GOVERN 4 (Risk culture)**: Organisational teams adopt a critical-thinking and safety-first mindset; document and communicate risks; enable incident identification and information sharing.
- **GOVERN 5 (External engagement)**: Policies collect, prioritise, and integrate feedback from external AI actors regarding societal impacts; mechanisms incorporate adjudicated feedback into design and implementation.
- **GOVERN 6 (Third-party and supply chain)**: Policies address IP rights, third-party AI risks, and contingency processes for high-risk third-party failures.
- **Risk culture**: Organisational attitude and norms that prioritise identification and management of AI risks; driven from senior leadership down through the organisation.

## Mental Models
- Treat GOVERN as the organisational substrate: MAP, MEASURE, and MANAGE cannot operate reliably without governance structures already in place.
- Use GOVERN before MAP: establish policies and accountability before beginning system-specific risk mapping.
- Treat GOVERN as continuous: governance obligations do not stop after initial setup; they must evolve as knowledge, culture, and AI actor expectations change.

## Anti-patterns
- **Delegating AI risk solely to technical teams**: GOVERN 2.3 requires executive leadership accountability; technical-only ownership creates governance gaps.
- **Ignoring third-party risks**: GOVERN 6 recognises that third-party data, models, and software introduce risks that the deploying organisation must manage regardless of where the component originated.
- **Treating AI governance as a project, not a programme**: GOVERN is a continuous function, not a one-time compliance exercise.

## Key Takeaways
1. GOVERN is the only function that applies to the entire AI lifecycle and the entire organisation; it must be in place before the other three functions are effective.
2. Senior executive accountability (GOVERN 2.3) is a non-negotiable requirement for effective AI risk governance.
3. AI system inventory (GOVERN 1.6) is foundational: you cannot manage risks from systems you have not catalogued.
4. Team diversity (GOVERN 3.1) is a governance requirement, not an HR aspiration; it directly shapes the quality of MAP, MEASURE, and MANAGE outputs.
5. Decommissioning planning (GOVERN 1.7) must be built into the lifecycle from the start, not added retroactively.
6. External feedback mechanisms (GOVERN 5) must be adjudicated and incorporated, not merely collected.

## Connects To
- **MAP function**: GOVERN policies and structures enable and constrain the context-setting activities in MAP.
- **MEASURE function**: GOVERN compliance and evaluation requirements are integrated into measurement processes.
- **MANAGE function**: GOVERN defines the resource allocation authority and risk response governance exercised in MANAGE.
- **ISO/IEC 42001**: AI management system standard whose governance requirements align with GOVERN categories.
- **NIST RMF SP 800-37**: Broader enterprise risk management framework whose accountability and authorisation structures parallel GOVERN 2.
