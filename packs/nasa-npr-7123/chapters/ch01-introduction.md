# Chapter 1: Introduction

## Core Idea
NPR 7123.1D establishes the mandatory systems engineering processes and requirements for all NASA programs and projects, providing a common technical process framework — the "SE Engine" — that is systematic, disciplined, quantifiable, recursive, iterative, and repeatable across the full system life cycle.

## Frameworks Introduced
- **SE Framework (Three Elements)**: The integrated combination of common technical processes, tools and methods, and a skilled workforce that together define and strengthen NASA's SE capability.
  - When to use: As the organising model when standing up or assessing an SE program; the three elements must all be present for effective SE capability.
- **Four Pillars of Technical Excellence**: Clearly documented requirements/policies/procedures; effective training and development; balancing risk; and continuous communications — underpinned by personal and organisational accountability.
  - When to use: When evaluating the health of a NASA program's engineering culture or when diagnosing systemic failure modes.

## Key Concepts
- **Systems Engineering (SE)**: A systematic, disciplined, quantifiable approach — recursive, iterative, and repeatable — for developing, operating, maintaining, and disposing of systems integrated as a whole through the life cycle, within cost, schedule, and risk constraints.
- **Common Technical Processes**: The 17 processes mandated by this NPR, grouped into system design, product realization, and technical management categories; collectively called the "SE Engine."
- **Engineering Technical Authority (ETA)**: The delegated engineering authority responsible for approving processes, specifications, design rules, and best practices necessary to fulfil programmatic mission performance requirements; independent of the program/project budget line.
- **Tailoring**: Formally adjusting or seeking relief from SE NPR requirements via waiver or deviation, approved through the ETA process, with rationale documented in the SEMP.
- **Customizing**: Selecting *how* an SE requirement is implemented without changing *what* must be done; does not require waivers or deviations but must be documented in the SEMP.
- **NPR (NASA Procedural Requirement)**: A mandatory Agency-level directive that programs and projects must comply with or obtain approved tailoring from; this document is NPR 7123.1D.
- **Precedence order**: In conflict, the order is: Space Act; NPD 1000.0; NPD 1000.3; NPD 7120.4; then NPR 7123.1.
- **OCE (Office of the Chief Engineer)**: The NASA entity responsible for policy, oversight, and assessment of engineering and program/project management processes; steward of the ETA framework.

## Mental Models
- Use tailoring when a specific NPR requirement cannot be met and you need formal relief; use customizing when you are choosing *how* to implement a requirement in a project-appropriate way.
- When SE capability is weak, diagnose against the three elements: are the processes defined? Are the tools available? Is the workforce trained? All three must be addressed together.
- Treat figures in this NPR as informational only; all normative content is in the numbered paragraphs.

## Anti-patterns
- **Confusing tailoring with customizing**: Tailoring requires ETA-approved waivers or deviations; customizing does not. Treating a customization choice as if it needed formal relief wastes time; skipping waivers for genuine tailoring is a compliance failure.
- **Sacrificing engineering rigor for schedule**: The ETA bears responsibility for ensuring risks are properly balanced; schedule pressure does not override the need for independent technical checks.

## Key Takeaways
1. NPR 7123.1D is the Agency-level SE process mandate; all programs and projects must comply or obtain approved tailoring.
2. The SE capability rests on three equal pillars: common processes, tools/methods, and trained workforce; weakness in any one undermines the others.
3. Technical Excellence requires not just documented procedures but also personal accountability, open communication, and deliberate risk balancing.
4. ETA independence is structural: ETAs are funded independently of programs to provide genuine checks and balances.
5. Figures in the NPR are informational; the enforceable content is in the numbered "shall" statements.

## Connects To
- **NPR 7120.5 (Space Flight Program and Project Management Requirements)**: Defines program/project life cycles and management reviews that NPR 7123.1 SE reviews align to.
- **NPR 7150.2 (NASA Software Engineering Requirements)**: Elaborates SE requirements specifically for software; technical teams must ensure software systems comply with both.
- **NASA/SP-6105 (NASA Systems Engineering Handbook)**: Non-mandatory companion handbook providing best practices, annotated outlines, and guidance for implementing the 17 processes required by this NPR.
