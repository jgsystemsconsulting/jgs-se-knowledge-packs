# Chapter 9: Appendix G — Life-Cycle and Technical Review Entrance and Success Criteria

## Core Idea
Appendix G provides detailed guidance tables of recommended entrance and success criteria for every life-cycle review type, distinguishing between program-level and project-level reviews, and focusing each set of criteria on three assessment dimensions: technical maturity, adequacy of planning (budget, schedule, risk credibility), and readiness to proceed to the next phase.

## Frameworks Introduced
- **Three-Dimension Review Assessment Framework**: Every life-cycle review is evaluated against (1) acceptable technical maturity, (2) adequacy of technical planning and credibility of budget, schedule, and risks, and (3) readiness to proceed to the next phase. Entrance and success criteria are written to demonstrate these three dimensions.
  - When to use: When writing or evaluating custom entrance/success criteria; all custom criteria should still address these three dimensions.
- **Program vs. Project Review Distinction**: Programs (except single-project programs) use Appendix G Tables G-1 and G-2 for their SRR and SDR; projects and single-project programs use Tables G-3 onwards. Different maturity expectations apply at each level.
  - When to use: When planning a multi-level review structure; the level of the entity being reviewed (program vs. project) determines which criteria table applies.
- **Starred Product Notation**: Required products are marked with asterisks: `**` = required by NPR 7123.1; `*` = required for NPR 7120.5 programs/projects; `***` = required by NPD 2570.5 (spectrum); `****` = cybersecurity per NPR 2810.1.
  - When to use: When building a review compliance checklist; the notation distinguishes mandatory NPR requirements from program-management-level requirements.

## Key Concepts
- **Entrance Criteria**: Minimum accomplishments a program/project must fulfil *before* a life-cycle review is convened; do not provide a complete list of all required products, but focus on readiness to begin the review productively.
- **Success Criteria**: Specific accomplishments that must be satisfactorily demonstrated *during or as a result of* the review for it to be considered successfully complete.
- **TBD/TBR Tracking**: To-Be-Determined (TBD) and To-Be-Resolved (TBR) items must be clearly identified at each review with acceptable plans and schedules for closure; success criteria include assessing TBD/TBR disposition.
- **Review Discrepancy Disposition**: Entrance criteria typically require that all RFAs, RIDs, and Action Items from prior reviews have been addressed and resolved, or that a timely closure plan exists for those remaining open.
- **Tailoring of Criteria Expected**: The decision not to tailor and customise life-cycle review criteria should be *justified* to the ETA — the default expectation is that criteria will be customised for program/project size and complexity.
- **Program SRR Focus**: The program SRR ensures that the program's functional and performance requirements are properly formulated and correlated with Mission Directorate strategic objectives; requirements must be adequately levied on constituent projects with documented traceability.
- **Program SDR Focus**: The SDR evaluates credibility and responsiveness of the proposed program requirements/architecture to Mission Directorate requirements, the allocation of requirements to projects, and maturity of the mission/system definition; the success criterion includes that the maturity is "sufficient to begin preliminary design."
- **Spectrum Manager Involvement**: RF spectrum requirements must appear in review success criteria; the responsible Center spectrum manager must be notified at SRR and approval confirmed at later reviews; spectrum certification proceeds through four stages (Conceptual, Experimental, Developmental, Operational).
- **Cybersecurity in Review Criteria**: System security risks including cybersecurity (governed by NPR 2810.1) must be addressed in entrance criteria risk identification at every review; top risks must include system security with mitigation strategies.
- **Lessons Learned**: Success criteria include evidence that lessons learned from other projects and programs have been identified and addressed; this is a recurring requirement across all review types.

## Mental Models
- Entrance criteria answer: "Are we ready to start the review?" Success criteria answer: "Did the review demonstrate what it was supposed to?" Conflating them wastes review board time.
- TBDs and TBRs at a review are not automatically disqualifying, but each must have an identified owner, disposition plan, and schedule; unplanned TBDs signal requirements immaturity.
- The starred notation distinguishes compliance sources: verify which starred items your program/project type is governed by before building your review checklist.
- Custom entrance/success criteria are the norm, not the exception; the Appendix G tables are starting points, not rigid checklists; the ETA validates the tailoring.

## Anti-patterns
- **Treating Appendix G tables as complete checklists**: The tables are recommended best practices; they do not list every product required at every review; additional programmatic products are required by governing NPRs.
- **Not involving the spectrum manager until late in the review cycle**: Spectrum requirements must be identified at SRR and tracked through each subsequent review; late identification creates schedule risk.
- **Closing a review before all review board report conditions are met**: A review is not complete until the review board chairperson reports to management, a decision memo is signed, and all action plan agreements are in place — not when the review meeting ends.

## Key Takeaways
1. Every entrance and success criterion targets one of three things: technical maturity, planning credibility, or readiness to proceed — custom criteria should do the same.
2. Programs and projects use different Appendix G tables; single-project programs follow the project tables (G-3 onwards), not the program tables (G-1, G-2).
3. TBD and TBR items must be explicitly planned for closure at every review; success criteria include assessing whether the disposition plans are acceptable and timely.
4. Tailoring and customising review criteria is expected; not tailoring must be justified to the ETA.
5. Spectrum management, cybersecurity, and lessons learned are recurring review criteria — they are not optional add-ons but are integrated into the entrance and success criteria at every gate.
6. The product notation (`, **, ***) is the compliance source indicator; teams should map each starred item to its governing directive before building the review package.
