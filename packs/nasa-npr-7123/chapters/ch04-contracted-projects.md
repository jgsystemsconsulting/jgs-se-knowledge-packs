# Chapter 4: NASA Systems Engineering Activities on Contracted Projects

## Core Idea
When work is contracted, the NASA technical team retains SE responsibilities across three phases — before award, during performance, and at completion — and must define, communicate, and exercise technical insight and oversight throughout, ensuring contracted efforts integrate seamlessly with the overall program/project SE approach.

## Frameworks Introduced
- **Insight vs. Oversight Distinction**: Insight is a monitoring activity (tracking contractor compliance through reports, reviews, surveys); oversight is an exercise of authority (Government retains and exercises the right to concur or non-concur with contractor decisions). Both are defined in the solicitation and SEMP.
  - When to use: When structuring the surveillance plan for a contract; risk posture determines the intensity mix of insight and oversight.
- **Three-Phase Contract SE Activity Structure**: Pre-award (define requirements, solicitation, proposal evaluation), During Performance (execute technical insight/oversight per contract), and Contract Completion (accept deliverables, manage product transition).
  - When to use: As the planning framework when the NASA technical team is standing up or managing a contracted program element.

## Key Concepts
- **Statement of Work (SOW)**: The technical narrative in the solicitation describing the work the contractor must perform, informed by knowledge of the 17 common technical processes.
- **Data Requirements List (DRL)**: The list of technical work products to be delivered by the contractor, including documentation specifying the contractor's SE approach to the 17 common technical processes.
- **Independent Government Estimate (IGE)**: A Government cost estimate developed as part of solicitation inputs; informs risk posture decisions about insight and oversight levels.
- **Surveillance Plan**: The Government's plan for monitoring contractor compliance; risk-posture-dependent; ranges from low-intensity (quarterly reports) to high-intensity (surveys and formal reviews).
- **Insight**: A monitoring activity — the Government tracks contractor compliance using Government-identified metrics and contracted milestones; does not require the Government to concur in contractor decisions.
- **Oversight**: An exercise of authority — the Government retains and exercises the right to concur or non-concur with contractor decisions; more resource-intensive than insight.
- **Contracting Officer**: The sole authority through whom NASA personnel may interface with the contractor; all technical insight and oversight activities operate under the contracting officer's authority.
- **Government Acceptance**: Formal acceptance of contractor deliverables at contract completion; the technical team must participate in the review(s) that finalise acceptance.

## Mental Models
- Use the 17 common technical processes as the framework for defining what contractor SE products and practices to require in the solicitation; the processes tell you what has to be done regardless of who does the work.
- Risk posture drives insight/oversight intensity: high-risk, safety-critical, or complex contracted elements warrant higher-intensity oversight; routine or low-risk elements may use lighter-touch insight.
- The NASA SEMP governs contracted work as much as in-house work: pre-award activities, surveillance plans, and product transition requirements all belong in the SEMP, not just the contract.

## Anti-patterns
- **Divulging requirements before solicitation release**: Pre-release disclosure of requirements or solicitation information violates acquisition regulations and compromises source selection integrity.
- **Technical team bypassing the contracting officer**: Authority to interface with the contractor is delegated only by the contracting officer; technical team members cannot exercise oversight authority directly.
- **Defining insight/oversight requirements after contract award**: These must be part of the solicitation so they are contractually binding; defining them after award requires costly contract modifications.

## Key Takeaways
1. The NASA technical team has mandatory SE responsibilities before, during, and after contracted work — contracting out work does not contract out technical accountability.
2. Insight and oversight are distinct: insight monitors; oversight directs. Both intensity levels and methods must be defined in the SEMP and the solicitation.
3. The 17 common technical processes inform every element of the solicitation: SOW tasks, DRL items, and proposal evaluation criteria.
4. All contractor-facing technical activities operate under the contracting officer's authority; the technical team must coordinate with the CO before acting.
5. Product transition at contract completion is a formal technical team requirement (SE-31), not an administrative afterthought.
