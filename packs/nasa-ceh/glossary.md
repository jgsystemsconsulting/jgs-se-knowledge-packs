# NASA Cost Estimating Handbook (CEH) Glossary

Key terms from the NASA Cost Estimating Handbook v4.0, alphabetical, with chapter references. Definitions are synthesized, not reproduced.

**Affordability Analysis** — a continuous, life-cycle process confirming a system can be delivered, owned, operated, and produced within established funding constraints while meeting approved requirements. (Ch 6)

**Agency Baseline Commitment (ABC)** — the external commitment NASA makes; policy sets it at a 70 percent JCL. (Ch 5)

**Analogy method** — estimating by adjusting the known cost of a similar fielded system up or down for differences; leans on expert opinion and still requires normalization. (Ch 3)

**Annual Cost Uncertainty** — a JCL product that time-phases the probabilistic cost-schedule risk results against available annual resources. (Ch 5)

**Basis of Estimate (BOE)** — the written justification (GR&A, drivers, model inputs, rationale) complete enough that an independent analyst could reproduce the estimate. (Ch 4)

**Beta Curve** — a technique for time-phasing an estimate against the planned development schedule. (Ch 4)

**CADRe (Cost Analysis Data Requirement)** — a three-part (programmatic, technical, cost) document produced six times across a project's life cycle to capture and archive historical data. (Ch 2, Ch 4)

**Cost As an Independent Variable (CAIV)** — a study process where design converges on a fixed cost target while still meeting threshold performance; commercial analog is target costing. (Ch 6)

**Cost Estimating Relationship (CER)** — a regression-derived equation linking cost (Y) to one or more cost-driver variables (X); valid only within its input data range. (Ch 3, Ch 4)

**Cost-Risk Assessment** — the six-activity Task 9 process that establishes the current confidence level and the reserve needed to reach a target confidence. (Ch 4)

**Cross Check** — a secondary estimate built from the same normalized data using a different model or technique to test reasonableness. (Ch 4)

**Cumulative Distribution Function (CDF) / S-curve** — the probabilistic cost curve spanning the project's range of costs, against which confidence levels are read. (Ch 4)

**Delphi method** — a structured, iterative expert-opinion technique used only when empirical data are absent; experts are typically anonymous. (Ch 3)

**Discount factor** — 1 / (1 + i)^n; the multiplier that converts a future cash flow to present value. (Ch 7)

**Discount rate** — the interest rate used in discounting; represents the opportunity cost of the investment (for government, its cost of borrowing). (Ch 7)

**Economic Analysis (EA)** — a discounted-cash-flow comparison of alternatives that weighs costs against benefits and produces measures of merit such as NPV. (Ch 7)

**Engineering build-up method** — decomposing the WBS into work packages and estimating each at the lowest level (labor and material separately), then aggregating; detailed and defensible but costly. (Ch 3)

**Earned Value Management (EVM)** — a performance-based technique for adjusting cost and schedule estimates against actual progress; a fourth data source once a program is underway. (Ch 3, Ch 4)

**Four Critical Elements** — resources, data, schedule, and expectations; jointly agreed between estimator and decision maker before a methodology is chosen. (Ch 2)

**Frontier line** — a line on the JCL scatterplot separating simulation runs that meet a chosen JCL from those that do not. (Ch 5)

**Ground Rules and Assumptions (GR&A)** — the global and element-specific bounds (dollars, schedule, inclusions/exclusions, quantities) documented continuously as they evolve. (Ch 3, Ch 4)

**Joint Cost and Schedule Confidence Level (JCL)** — the probability of finishing at or below both the cost target and the schedule target, jointly. (Ch 5)

**Lease-Versus-Buy Analysis** — an economic comparison of leasing versus owning an asset, governed by OMB Circular A-94; weighs lease vs. debt financing. (Ch 6)

**Life-Cycle Cost (LCC) point estimate** — the deterministic single-value estimate produced in Task 8 by running the model on normalized data; the start, not the end, of cost-risk work. (Ch 4)

**Make-Versus-Buy Analysis** — a strategic choice between producing an item in-house and outsourcing it. (Ch 6)

**Management Agreement (MA)** — a policy threshold set at no less than a 50 percent JCL. (Ch 5)

**Methodology (estimating)** — analogy, parametric, or engineering build-up; the choice is governed by data availability, which shifts across the life cycle. (Ch 3)

**NASA New Start Inflation Index (NNSI)** — NASA's annual inflation index for new efforts and normalizing prior-mission cost; not for civil-servant or already-contracted costs. (Ch 3, Ch 7)

**Net Present Value (NPV)** — the discounted sum of an alternative's net cash flows; the standard ranking criterion (most-positive NPV preferred). (Ch 7)

**Nominal vs. real rate** — nominal rates include inflation (use with current/then-year dollars); real rates exclude it (use with constant/base-year dollars). (Ch 7)

**Non-Advocate Review (NAR)** — a formal cost review where poorly documented analyses fare badly. (Ch 4)

**Normalization** — adjusting raw data (inflation, learning curves, production rate, scope, reporting differences) to make it comparable. (Ch 3)

**OMB Circular A-94** — the federal benefit-cost reference prescribing discount rates and establishing NPV as the standard justification criterion. (Ch 6, Ch 7)

**ONCE (One NASA Cost Engineering database)** — the repository storing completed CADRes and receiving end-of-contract actuals to update cost models. (Ch 2, Ch 3)

**Parametric method** — estimating via CERs on the assumption that past cost drivers will drive future cost; fast and replicable but loses credibility outside its data range. (Ch 3)

**Point estimate** — a deterministic estimate built from fixed most-likely inputs; the baseline against which sensitivity change is measured. (Ch 4, Ch 6)

**Probabilistic cost estimate** — the enhancement of a deterministic point estimate into a range of outcomes via simulation or analytical methods. (Ch 4)

**Probabilistic Cost-Loaded Schedule (PCLS)** — the primary method for producing a JCL: a logic-networked schedule with cost mapped to activities. (Ch 5)

**Program Cost Commitment (PCC)** — the commitment NPR 7120.4 requires be revalidated annually, eased by BOE documentation. (Ch 4)

**Real Year (RY) / then-year dollars** — dollars including inflation; NASA's "Real Year" usage is the opposite of DoD's (where "Real" means Constant). (Ch 3, Ch 4)

**Sensitivity Analysis** — a five-step, one-element-at-a-time technique to find which requirements move cost the most; gives no probabilities. (Ch 4, Ch 6)

**Standing Review Board (SRB)** — the non-advocacy body that evaluates the JCL. (Ch 5)

**Technical baseline / Technical Description** — the quantitative and qualitative description of the project that gives Project Office and independent estimators one common definition. (Ch 2)

**Time-Dependent (TD) cost** — cost tied to a task's duration (the "marching army"); grows when the schedule slips. (Ch 5)

**Time-Independent (TI) cost** — cost unaffected by duration (e.g., materials); schedule slips do not move it. (Ch 5)

**Trade Study (cost-performance)** — an interdisciplinary examination plotting alternatives in a trade space to find an optimal balance of performance and cost. (Ch 6)

**Trade space** — a multidimensional space bounded by cost and one or more performance parameters in which alternatives are plotted. (Ch 6)

**Unallocated Future Expense (UFE)** — the budgeted reserve quantified by risk analysis to reach a chosen confidence level. (Ch 4, Ch 5)

**Uncertainty** — the indefiniteness of the baseline plan itself, distinct from discrete risks; usually modeled with a triangular distribution. (Ch 5)

**Work Breakdown Structure (WBS)** — a product-oriented decomposition giving full coverage without double counting; mandated by NPR 7120.5E, standardized to Level 2. (Ch 2)
