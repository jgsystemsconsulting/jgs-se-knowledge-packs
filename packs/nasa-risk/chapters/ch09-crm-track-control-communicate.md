# Chapter 9: CRM — Track, Control, and Communicate & Document Steps

## Core Idea
Track monitors both implementation progress and the effectiveness of selected risk responses against pre-established observable criteria; Control evaluates tracking data and acts to keep risk responses on course or triggers a new Plan cycle; Communicate and Document permeates all CRM steps and ensures consistent, timely risk information flows across the organisational hierarchy.

## Frameworks Introduced
- **Performance Risk Tracking Chart**: A time-series chart showing how each performance risk evolves relative to its burn-down profile (Tolerable / Marginal / Intolerable zones) through the project milestones.
  - When to use: Throughout CRM Track — updated whenever tracking data are collected; the primary visual communication aid for risk posture over time.
- **Risk Communication Protocols**: Negotiated, documented agreements among organisational units on the frequency, content, format, and triggers for risk information reporting.
  - When to use: Established in the RMP at CRM initialisation; invoked continuously during Track, Control, and at every CRM cycle.
- **Relational Risk Database**: The central repository for all CRM documentation — individual risks, performance risk models, risk driver lists, risk response plans, tracking data, and decision rationale — with many-to-many linkages between entities.
  - When to use: Continuously throughout CRM; the database must be accessible to all affected organisational units and must support archiving of evolving risk analysis results.

## Key Concepts
- **Track Applicability by Disposition Type**: Track applies to Mitigate, Watch, and Research. Accept, Close, and Elevate have no tracking requirements because they do not involve ongoing implementation or monitoring.
- **Mitigate Tracking — Two Monitoring Streams**: (1) Implementation progress integrated into the project schedule; (2) Observable quantities related to mitigation effectiveness, compared against forecasted risk reduction and forecasted cost.
- **Watch Tracking**: Scheduled monitoring of observables related to risk drivers; tracked parameters serve as early warning indicators; contingency plans are invoked based on pre-established criteria.
- **Research Tracking**: Tracks implementation of the research plan and observables related to research findings; contingency plans invoked based on research results.
- **Actual vs. Forecasted Risk Reduction**: A key control comparison — if mitigation produces less risk reduction than the Plan step predicted, the gap must be explained and the integrated risk model updated.
- **Control Scope**: Control acts within the scope of the selected alternative. If control actions are insufficient to achieve the intended result, the Plan step is reinitiated.
- **Communication Scope in CRM**: All affected units must be informed of: individual risks affecting their performance risk; consistent modelling assumptions; risk driver lists (updated on schedule); planning activities for shared risk drivers; implemented risk responses affecting their analysis; and timely elevation decisions.
- **Risk Driver List**: A living communication aid shared across units, showing the current set of risk drivers, their owning units, and their disposition status.
- **Documentation in the Risk Database**: Must capture risk analysis results as they evolve, risk response planning history, the selected alternative and its rationale, and Risk Response Documents (RRDs) generated for each risk response decision.

## Mental Models
- Think of Track as two parallel stopwatches: one tracking whether the mitigation is being implemented on schedule, the other tracking whether it is actually reducing the risk drivers it targeted.
- Control is the feedback loop that closes the gap between planned and actual risk reduction; it acts first within the selected alternative's scope before escalating back to Plan.
- Communication protocols should be negotiated, not assumed — what information, at what frequency, in what format, to which organisational units, must be explicitly agreed in the RMP.
- The risk database is not a filing system — it is the single source of truth for all risk management decisions; its relational structure (many-to-many links) is what enables cross-cutting analysis and audit trails.

## Anti-patterns
- **Monitoring only implementation, not effectiveness**: Confirming that a mitigation was executed does not confirm that it reduced the risk driver it targeted; both streams must be tracked.
- **Allowing control to drift into informal management decisions**: Actions taken outside the documented Control framework are not reflected in the risk model, creating unacknowledged divergence between the model and reality.
- **Batch risk reporting only at milestones**: Risk communication must also include trigger-based reporting when elevation thresholds are exceeded — waiting for the next milestone wastes the window for effective response.
- **Treating documentation as retrospective**: The risk database must be maintained in real time; retrospective entry of risk decisions after the fact defeats the audit function and corrupts the archival record.

## Key Takeaways
1. Track serves two distinct purposes — monitoring implementation and monitoring effectiveness — and both must be active simultaneously for each Mitigate response.
2. Control acts within the selected alternative's scope before re-invoking Plan; escalating to Plan prematurely wastes cycle time, but waiting too long allows risk to become unmanageable.
3. Communication and documentation are not separate CRM steps — they are integrated into every step; Communicate and Document is the connective tissue of the whole process.
4. Risk communication protocols must be negotiated and documented in the RMP; undocumented protocols fail when personnel change or organisational boundaries shift.
5. The relational risk database is the institutional memory of the RM process; its many-to-many structure is essential for linking individual risks, risk drivers, performance risks, and responses across organisational units.
