# MIL-STD-882E Glossary

**Acceptable Risk** — Risk that the appropriate acceptance authority (as defined in applicable DoDI 5000 series) is willing to accept without additional mitigation.

**Acquisition Program** — A directed, funded effort that provides a new, improved, or continuing materiel, weapon, or information system or service capability in response to an approved need.

**Autonomous (AT) — SCC 1** — Software control category in which software exercises autonomous control authority over potentially safety-significant hardware without the possibility of predetermined safe detection and intervention to preclude a mishap.

**Causal Factor** — One or several mechanisms that trigger a hazard that may result in a mishap.

**Civilian Harm** — Civilian casualties (death or injury of civilians) and damage to or destruction of civilian objects not constituting military objectives under the law of war resulting from military operations; also includes adverse effects on civilian population and infrastructure.

**COTS (Commercial-Off-the-Shelf)** — Commercial items requiring no unique Government modifications over the system life-cycle.

**Design Order of Precedence** — The five-level hierarchy of mitigation selection strategies ordered by decreasing effectiveness: (1) eliminate via design selection, (2) reduce risk via design alteration, (3) incorporate engineered features or devices, (4) provide warning devices, (5) incorporate signage, procedures, training, and PPE.

**Eliminated (F) — Probability Level** — Incapable of occurrence; used when potential hazards are identified and later physically eliminated. Cannot be achieved through procedures, training, warnings, cautions, or PPE.

**Environmental Impact** — An adverse change to the environment wholly or partially caused by the system or its use.

**ESOH** — Environment, Safety, and Occupational Health; the combined disciplines addressing environmental compliance, system safety, occupational safety and health, and hazardous materials management.

**Event Risk** — The risk associated with a hazard as it applies to a specific hardware/software configuration during a specific event (DT/OT, demonstration, fielding, post-fielding tests).

**EHA (Environmental Hazard Analysis)** — Task 210; identifies and manages environmental hazards across all life-cycle phases; supports NEPA and EO 12114 requirements.

**FHA (Functional Hazard Analysis)** — Task 208; identifies and classifies system functions and the safety consequences of their failure or malfunction; identifies SCFs, SCIs, SRFs, SRIs, and SSSFs; assigns SCC and SwCI.

**Firmware** — The combination of a hardware device and computer instructions or data that reside as read-only software on the hardware device; not readily modified under programme control; included in MIL-STD-882E definition of software.

**Frequent (A) — Probability Level** — Likely to occur often in the life of an item; continuously experienced in fleet. Quantitative: >= 10^-1.

**GFE (Government-Furnished Equipment)** — Property in Government possession subsequently delivered to the contractor for use.

**GFI (Government-Furnished Information)** — Information in Government possession subsequently delivered to the contractor for use.

**GOTS (Government-Off-the-Shelf)** — Hardware or software developed, produced, or owned by a government agency requiring no unique modification over the system life-cycle.

**Hazard** — A real or potential condition that could lead to an unplanned event or series of events (mishap) resulting in death, injury, occupational illness, damage to or loss of equipment or property, or damage to the environment.

**HAZMAT (Hazardous Material)** — Any item or substance that, due to its chemical, physical, toxicological, or biological nature, could cause harm to people, equipment, or the environment.

**HHA (Health Hazard Analysis)** — Task 207; evaluates human health hazards from chemical, physical, biological, ergonomic, and radiation stressors; includes HAZMAT evaluation and operational environment characterisation.

**HMAR (Hazard Management Assessment Report)** — Task 302; comprehensive multi-discipline evaluation of hazard status and associated risks prior to test, next contract phase, or contract completion.

**HMP (Hazard Management Plan)** — Task 103; planning document covering system safety methodology across multiple SE functional disciplines; alternative to the SSPP for multi-discipline programmes.

**HMMP (Hazardous Materials Management Plan)** — Task 108; defines roles, responsibilities, and procedures for HAZMAT identification, categorisation, tracking, and reporting.

**HSI (Human Systems Integration)** — The integrated and comprehensive analysis, design, and assessment of requirements for system manpower, personnel, training, safety, occupational health, habitability, personnel survivability, and human factors engineering.

**HTS (Hazard Tracking System)** — The closed-loop database that records all hazard data, risk assessments, mitigation status, verification records, and formal risk acceptances throughout the system life-cycle.

**Improbable (E) — Probability Level** — So unlikely it can be assumed occurrence may not be experienced in item life; unlikely to occur but possible in fleet. Quantitative: < 10^-6.

**Initial Risk** — The first assessment of potential risk of an identified hazard; establishes a fixed baseline for the hazard.

**IV&V (Independent Verification and Validation)** — Independent assessment of software separate from the developing team; referenced in Appendix B as a test task consideration for safety-significant software.

**Level of Rigor (LOR)** — Specification of the depth and breadth of software analysis and verification activities necessary to provide sufficient confidence that a safety-critical or safety-related software function will perform as required.

**Life-cycle** — All phases of the system's life: design, research, development, test and evaluation, production, deployment, operations and support, and disposal.

**Mishap** — An event or series of events resulting in unintentional death, injury, occupational illness, damage to or loss of equipment or property, or damage to the environment; includes negative environmental impacts from planned events.

**Mitigation Measure** — Action required to eliminate the hazard or, when a hazard cannot be eliminated, reduce the associated risk by lessening severity or lowering the likelihood of a mishap.

**Monetary Loss** — Sum of estimated costs for equipment repair/replacement, facility repair/replacement, environmental cleanup, personal injury or illness, environmental liabilities, and known fines or penalties from a projected mishap.

**NDI (Non-Developmental Item)** — Items (hardware, software, communications/networks) used in system development but not developed as part of the programme; includes COTS, GOTS, GFE, re-use items.

**Negligible — Severity Category IV** — Could result in injury/illness not causing a lost work day, minimal environmental impact, or monetary loss < $100K.

**NSI (No Safety Impact) — SCC 5** — Software that has no command or control authority over safety-significant hardware, provides no safety-significant information, and does not transport or resolve safety-significant or time-sensitive communication.

**O&SHA (Operating and Support Hazard Analysis)** — Task 206; identifies and assesses hazards introduced by operational and support activities; treats the human as a system element.

**Occasional (C) — Probability Level** — Likely to occur sometime in item life; will occur several times in fleet. Quantitative: >= 10^-3 and < 10^-2.

**PHA (Preliminary Hazard Analysis)** — Task 202; first formal risk assessment; assigns initial RACs to identified hazards using best available data; identifies potential mitigations.

**PHL (Preliminary Hazard List)** — Task 201; earliest hazard identification task; compiles potential hazards from legacy/similar system data.

**PM (Program Manager)** — Designated Government individual with responsibility and authority to accomplish programme objectives; accountable for credible cost, schedule, and performance reporting to the Milestone Decision Authority.

**Probable (B) — Probability Level** — Will occur several times in item life; will occur frequently in fleet. Quantitative: >= 10^-2 and < 10^-1.

**Probability** — An expression of the likelihood of occurrence of a mishap.

**RAC (Risk Assessment Code)** — Alphanumeric combining severity category (1–4) and probability level (A–F) to express combined risk; e.g., 1A = Catastrophic/Frequent (High risk).

**Remote (D) — Probability Level** — Unlikely but possible in item life; unlikely but can reasonably be expected in fleet. Quantitative: >= 10^-6 and < 10^-3.

**RFT (Redundant Fault Tolerant) — SCC 3** — Software that issues commands requiring redundant, independent fault-tolerant mechanisms for each hazardous condition, with adequate fault detection, annunciation, tolerance, and recovery.

**Risk** — A combination of the severity of the mishap and the probability that the mishap will occur.

**Risk Level** — Characterisation of risk as High, Serious, Medium, or Low based on RAC per Table III.

**Safety** — Freedom from conditions that can cause death, injury, occupational illness, damage to or loss of equipment or property, or damage to the environment.

**Safety Assessment Report (SAR)** — Task 301; comprehensive evaluation of hazard status prior to test, next contract phase, or contract completion; includes a readiness statement.

**Safety-Critical** — Applied to a condition, event, operation, process, or item whose mishap severity consequence is Catastrophic (I) or Critical (II).

**Safety-Critical Function (SCF)** — A function whose failure to operate or incorrect operation will directly result in a Catastrophic or Critical mishap.

**Safety-Critical Item (SCI)** — A hardware or software item that may contribute to a Catastrophic or Critical hazard or that mitigates one.

**Safety Release** — T&E document communicating known hazards, test-unique mitigations, and risk acceptance status to test personnel before a test event.

**Safety-Related** — Applied to a condition, event, operation, process, or item whose mishap severity consequence is Marginal (III) or Negligible (IV).

**Safety-Significant** — Applied to anything that is either safety-critical or safety-related.

**SAT (Semi-Autonomous) — SCC 2** — Software that exercises control authority over safety-significant hardware, allowing time for independent safety mechanisms to mitigate or control mishaps.

**SCC (Software Control Category)** — Assignment of the degree of autonomy, command and control authority, and redundant fault tolerance of a software function in context with system behaviour. Levels 1–5 (AT through NSI).

**Semi-Autonomous (SAT) — SCC 2** — See SAT.

**Severity** — The magnitude of potential consequences of a mishap: death, injury, occupational illness, equipment/property damage, environmental damage, or monetary loss.

**SHA (System Hazard Analysis)** — Task 205; analyses hazards at the integrated system level including all subsystem interfaces; examines common cause failures, simultaneous events, and human errors.

**SoS (System-of-Systems)** — A set or arrangement of interdependent systems that are related or connected to provide a given capability.

**SoSHA (System-of-Systems Hazard Analysis)** — Task 209; identifies unique SoS hazards that emerge from system interactions not present in any constituent system alone.

**SRHA (System Requirements Hazard Analysis)** — Task 203; identifies design requirements to eliminate hazards or reduce risks; flows them into system specifications; assesses compliance at design reviews.

**SSPP (System Safety Program Plan)** — Task 102; primary system safety planning document; describes the contractor's approach to system safety as an integral part of the SEMP.

**SSCM (Software Safety Criticality Matrix)** — Table V; 5x4 matrix of SCC (rows) crossed with severity category (columns) producing SwCI 1–5; drives required LOR tasks.

**SSHA (Subsystem Hazard Analysis)** — Task 204; verifies subsystem compliance with requirements; identifies previously unidentified hazards at the component level.

**SSSF (Safety-Significant Software Function)** — A software function that can cause, contribute to, or influence a safety-significant hazard; requires SCC assignment and SwCI rating.

**SwCI (Software Criticality Index)** — A number (1–5) assigned from the SSCM; drives required LOR task depth; SwCI 1 = highest rigor; SwCI 5 = no safety analysis required.

**System** — The organisation of hardware, software, material, facilities, personnel, data, and services needed to perform a designated function in a stated environment with specified results.

**System Safety** — The application of engineering and management principles, criteria, and techniques to achieve acceptable risk within the constraints of operational effectiveness, suitability, time, and cost throughout all phases of the system life-cycle.

**System Safety Engineering** — Engineering discipline that identifies hazards and eliminates them or reduces associated risks.

**System Safety Management** — All plans and actions taken to identify hazards; assess and mitigate associated risks; and track, control, accept, and document risks across the system life-cycle.

**Target Risk** — The projected risk level the PM plans to achieve by implementing mitigation measures consistent with the design order of precedence.

**User Representative** — For fielding events, the Command or agency formally designated in JCIDS to represent users; for non-fielding events, the Command/agency responsible for personnel, equipment, and environment exposed to risk; must provide formal concurrence before all Serious and High risk acceptance decisions.
