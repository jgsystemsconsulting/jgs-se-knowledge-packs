# Chapter 1: Scope, Purpose & Key Definitions

## Core Idea
MIL-STD-882E w/Change 1 (27 September 2023) is the DoD-wide standard practice for system safety. Its purpose is to provide a standard, generic method for identifying, classifying, and mitigating hazards across the full life-cycle of any defense system — hardware, software, infrastructure — from design through disposal. When cited in a contract without specific task selection, only Sections 3 and 4 are mandatory.

## Key Concepts

- **Hazard** — a real or potential condition that could lead to an unplanned event (mishap) resulting in death, injury, occupational illness, damage/loss to equipment or property, or environmental damage.
- **Mishap** — an event or series of events resulting in unintentional death, injury, occupational illness, damage/loss to equipment or property, or environmental damage. Includes negative environmental impacts from planned events.
- **Risk** — a combination of the severity of the mishap and the probability that the mishap will occur.
- **Safety** — freedom from conditions that can cause death, injury, occupational illness, damage to or loss of equipment or property, or damage to the environment.
- **System safety** — the application of engineering and management principles, criteria, and techniques to achieve acceptable risk within the constraints of operational effectiveness and suitability, time, and cost throughout all phases of the system life-cycle.
- **System safety engineering** — the engineering discipline that employs specialized knowledge to identify hazards and eliminate them or reduce associated risks.
- **System safety management** — all plans and actions taken to identify hazards; assess and mitigate associated risks; and track, control, accept, and document risks across the system life-cycle.
- **Acceptable risk** — risk that the appropriate acceptance authority (as defined in applicable DoDI 5000 series) is willing to accept without additional mitigation.
- **Causal factor** — one or several mechanisms that trigger the hazard that may result in a mishap.
- **Mitigation measure** — action required to eliminate the hazard or, when a hazard cannot be eliminated, reduce the associated risk by lessening severity or lowering the likelihood of a mishap.
- **Life-cycle** — all phases of the system's life: design, research, development, test and evaluation, production, deployment, operations and support, and disposal.
- **Safety-critical** — applied to a condition, event, operation, process, or item whose mishap severity consequence is Catastrophic (I) or Critical (II).
- **Safety-related** — applied to a condition, event, operation, process, or item whose mishap severity consequence is Marginal (III) or Negligible (IV).
- **Safety-significant** — applied to anything that is either safety-critical or safety-related.
- **System-of-Systems (SoS)** — a set or arrangement of interdependent systems that are related or connected to provide a given capability.
- **Event risk** — the risk associated with a hazard for a specific hardware/software configuration during a specific event (DT/OT, demonstration, fielding, post-fielding test).
- **Target risk** — the projected risk level the PM plans to achieve by implementing mitigation measures consistent with the design order of precedence.
- **Initial risk** — the first assessment of potential risk of an identified hazard; establishes a fixed baseline.
- **ESOH** — Environment, Safety, and Occupational Health; the combination of disciplines addressing environmental compliance, system safety, occupational safety and health, and hazardous materials management.

## Key Takeaways

1. MIL-STD-882E applies to all DoD systems across all life-cycle phases — it is a process standard, not a design standard.
2. Without specific task imposition, only Sections 3 (definitions) and 4 (general requirements) are contractually mandatory.
3. Risk is defined as the combination of severity AND probability — not either dimension alone.
4. Safety-critical (Cat I/II) and safety-related (Cat III/IV) are formally defined terms with distinct consequence levels and different design obligations.
5. The standard is intended for all SE functional disciplines (fire protection, occupational health, environmental engineering), not only dedicated system safety personnel.
6. Mitigation goals follow a strict priority: eliminate the hazard first; reduce risk only when elimination is infeasible.
