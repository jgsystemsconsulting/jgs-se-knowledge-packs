# GAO Technology Readiness Assessment Guide Cheatsheet

## Quick Decision Rules

**"Is this technology a CTE?"**
Both must be true: (1) it is **new or novel**, or used in a **new or novel way**; AND (2) it is **needed** for the system to meet its operational performance requirements within cost/schedule. → If both yes, it's a CTE and gets assessed. If either no, screen it out (and document why).

**"What TRL is this, really?"**
Ask what was demonstrated, **in what environment**, on what evidence. Match the environment honestly:
- Laboratory only → TRL ≤ 4
- Relevant environment → TRL 5–6
- Operational environment → TRL 7+
If the demonstration was in a softer environment than claimed, the TRL is inflated.

**"Is this TRA reliable enough to act on?"**
Test the four characteristics — Comprehensive · Well-documented · Credible · Defensible. A "no" on any one discounts the whole.

**"Is the technology mature enough to commit?"**
Sufficiency is relative to the decision. Best-practice thresholds: **TRL 6** (relevant env) by preliminary design; **TRL 7** (operational env) before committing to product development.

**"A CTE is immature — now what?"**
Build a **Technology Maturation Plan**: target TRL + decision, activities, resources, schedule, off-ramps. Track as a risk burn-down.

## Technology Readiness Levels (TRL 1–9)

| TRL | Meaning | Environment |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Basic principles observed | — |
| 2 | Concept/application formulated | — |
| 3 | Analytical/experimental proof-of-concept | Laboratory |
| 4 | Component/breadboard validated | Laboratory |
| 5 | Component/breadboard validated | **Relevant** |
| 6 | System/subsystem prototype demonstrated | **Relevant** |
| 7 | System prototype demonstrated | **Operational** |
| 8 | Actual system completed & qualified | Operational |
| 9 | Actual system mission-proven | Operational |

**Decisive jumps**: 4→5/6 (lab → relevant) and 6→7 (relevant → operational). Companion scales: **MRL** (producibility), **IRL** (integration).

## The Five-Step TRA Process

1. **Prepare the TRA plan & select the team** — scope, criteria, environments, independent expert team.
2. **Identify the CTEs** — WBS/requirements + the "new/novel + needed" test.
3. **Assess CTE readiness** — evidence first, then assign TRL against criteria/environment.
4. **Prepare the TRA report** — traceable, repeatable: CTEs, evidence, TRLs, environment, assumptions, dissent.
5. **Use the results** — feed decisions; trigger a TMP for immature CTEs.

## The Four Characteristics of a Reliable TRA

| Characteristic | Means | Fails when… |
|---|---|---|
| **Comprehensive** | all CTEs, full criteria, right environment | a CTE is missed / coverage is partial |
| **Well-documented** | traceable, repeatable evidence & rationale | bare TRL number, no basis |
| **Credible** | qualified, independent assessors; sufficient evidence | developers self-assess; thin evidence |
| **Defensible** | conclusions follow from evidence, survive scrutiny | ratings collapse under challenge |

The four form a chain — documentation makes the other three checkable.

## Technology Maturation Plan (TMP) — contents

Current TRL · Target TRL + the decision it supports · Maturation activities/tests · Resources & funding · Realistic schedule with milestones · Risks · **Off-ramps / alternatives**. Size by the TRL gap; track milestones like a risk burn-down.

## Tells & Smells

- **TRL with no environment named** → likely inflated; demand "demonstrated where?"
- **Developer rated their own technology** → not credible; needs independent assessment.
- **TRL number with no evidence trail** → not well-documented; an assertion, not an assessment.
- **All technologies "assessed"** → CTE screen probably skipped; the critical few are buried.
- **A novel-use mature component marked non-critical** → missed CTE; criticality is contextual.
- **Calendar-driven TRA** ("rating due Friday") → timed to a date, not to evidence.
- **Immature CTE, no TMP / no off-ramp** → unmanaged risk betting the program on one technology.
- **Committing to development below TRL 7** → classic cost-growth / schedule-slip setup.
