# GAO Technology Readiness Assessment Guide Glossary

Key terms from the GAO TRA Guide (GAO-20-48G, 2020), alphabetical, with chapter references.

**Comprehensive (TRA characteristic)** — all CTEs identified and assessed against complete criteria in the correct environment. (Ch 5)

**Credible (TRA characteristic)** — assessment performed by qualified, independent assessors using sufficient, objective evidence. (Ch 5)

**Critical Technology Element (CTE)** — a technology that is new or novel (or used in a new or novel way) AND needed for the system to meet its operational performance requirements within cost and schedule. (Ch 3)

**Defensible (TRA characteristic)** — conclusions follow logically from the evidence and withstand independent scrutiny. (Ch 5)

**Environment (laboratory / relevant / operational)** — the fidelity of the conditions in which a technology has been demonstrated; the basis for distinguishing TRLs. (Ch 2)

**Integration Readiness Level (IRL)** — a scale for how well system elements work together; complements TRL where integration is the binding risk. (Ch 2)

**Knowledge-based acquisition** — the principle that major investment commitments should be made only when the requisite knowledge, including technology maturity, is in hand. (Ch 7, Ch 1)

**Manufacturing Readiness Level (MRL)** — a scale for producibility (can it be made affordably at rate?); complements TRL. (Ch 2)

**Operational environment** — the real (or fully representative) environment the system will operate in; demonstration here supports TRL 7+. (Ch 2)

**Relevant environment** — an environment reproducing the key stresses the technology will face; demonstration here supports TRL 5–6. (Ch 2)

**Technology Maturation Plan (TMP)** — the program's documented plan to raise an immature CTE to the required TRL, with activities, resources, schedule, and off-ramps. (Ch 6)

**Technology Readiness Assessment (TRA)** — a systematic, evidence-based, point-in-time evaluation of the maturity of critical technologies, expressed in TRLs; a risk-identification tool, not a pass/fail gate. (Ch 1)

**Technology Readiness Level (TRL)** — a 1–9 scale expressing demonstrated technology maturity, keyed to environment fidelity. (Ch 2)

**TRA report** — the documented product of the assessment: CTEs, evidence, TRLs, environments, assumptions, limitations, and dissent. (Ch 4, Ch 5)

**TRL 1–9** — basic principles (1) → concept (2) → proof-of-concept (3) → component in lab (4) → component in relevant env (5) → prototype in relevant env (6) → prototype in operational env (7) → system qualified (8) → system mission-proven (9). (Ch 2)

**Well-documented (TRA characteristic)** — evidence, methods, assumptions, and TRL rationale captured so the assessment can be traced and repeated. (Ch 5)

**Work Breakdown Structure (WBS)** — the hierarchical decomposition of the system used (with requirements) to identify candidate CTEs. (Ch 3)
