# Chapter 4: The Five-Step TRA Process

## Core Idea
A high-quality TRA follows a repeatable five-step process: **plan and staff** the assessment, **identify** the critical technology elements, **assess** their readiness against evidence, **report** the findings, and **use** the results in decisions and maturation planning. Each step has best practices, and Appendix II of the Guide provides "key questions" used to judge how well a program executed them.

## Frameworks Introduced
- **The five-step TRA process** — Plan & team → Identify CTEs → Assess readiness → Report → Use results.
  - When to use: to structure any TRA and to audit one after the fact.
- **Independence of the assessment team** — the people assessing readiness should be qualified and independent of the developers whose technology is rated.
  - When to use: when forming the team in step 1; independence is a precondition for credibility (ch05).
- **Appendix II key questions** — a checklist GAO auditors apply to each step to evaluate TRA quality.
  - When to use: to self-assess or critique a TRA against best practice.

## Key Concepts — the five steps
1. **Prepare the TRA plan and select the assessment team.** Define scope, objectives, methodology, schedule, and TRL criteria/environments up front. Select an **independent, credible, expert** team with the right domain and assessment skills. The plan is the contract for how the TRA will be run.
2. **Identify the Critical Technology Elements.** Apply the CTE test ("new/novel or novel use AND needed for operational requirements") systematically from the WBS and requirements; have the list independently reviewed (see ch03).
3. **Assess the CTEs' readiness.** Gather and evaluate **evidence** for each CTE, then assign a TRL against the defined criteria and environment. Evidence — not assertion — drives the rating; document what is missing or assumed.
4. **Prepare the TRA report.** Document each CTE, the evidence considered, the assigned TRL, the environment of demonstration, assumptions, limitations, and dissent. The report must be traceable enough to be repeated and scrutinized.
5. **Use the TRA results.** Feed maturity findings into the program decision; for any CTE below the required TRL, trigger a **Technology Maturation Plan** (ch06). A TRA whose results are filed and ignored has wasted its effort.

## Mental Models
- The five steps are a pipeline, and step 2 (CTE identification) is the choke point: everything downstream assesses only what step 2 captured.
- Independence is designed in at step 1, not bolted on at step 4: you cannot make a non-independent assessment credible by writing a nicer report.
- Evidence first, number second: in step 3, assemble the evidence and let the TRL fall out of it — never pick the TRL and back-fill the evidence.
- The report (step 4) is the durable product: if the assessment cannot be traced and repeated from the report, it is not well-documented (ch05) and will not be defensible.

## Anti-patterns
- **Non-independent assessment**: developers rating their own technology, or a team with a stake in the outcome.
- **Skipping the plan**: assessing without agreed scope, criteria, and environments, so ratings are inconsistent and disputable.
- **Number-first assessment**: choosing the desired TRL and assembling supporting evidence afterward.
- **Filed-and-forgotten**: completing the report (step 4) but never feeding results into decisions or a TMP (step 5).

## Key Takeaways
1. The process has **five steps**: plan & team, identify CTEs, assess readiness, report, use results.
2. **Step 1** sets scope, criteria, environments, and an **independent, expert team** — independence is designed in here.
3. **Step 2 (CTE identification)** is the choke point — everything downstream assesses only what it captures.
4. **Step 3** is **evidence-first**: assemble evidence, then assign the TRL — never the reverse.
5. **Step 4** produces a **traceable, repeatable** report including assumptions, limitations, and dissent.
6. **Step 5** uses results in decisions and triggers a TMP for immature CTEs — results unused are effort wasted.
7. **Appendix II key questions** let you audit how well each step was executed.
