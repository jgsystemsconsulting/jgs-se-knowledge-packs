# Chapter 1: Introduction, Purpose & Vision for Digital Engineering

## Core Idea
The DoD is shifting its engineering practice from a document-centric, sequential way of building weapon systems to **digital engineering** — an integrated, model-based approach that treats authoritative system data and models as a connected continuum spanning every discipline and every lifecycle phase, from concept through disposal. The aim is to keep delivering decisive capability to the warfighter faster, despite accelerating technology change, rising system complexity, tight budgets, and compressed schedules. The Strategy frames this as both a method change (new methods, processes, and tools) and a culture change reaching well beyond the engineering shop.

## Frameworks Introduced
- **DoD Digital Engineering Strategy (2018)** — the governing document this pack is built on, authored by the Office of the Deputy Assistant Secretary of Defense for Systems Engineering, ODASD(SE), with stakeholders across government, industry, and academia.
  - When/how: it is positioned as a *living document* meant to evolve, and explicitly **not prescriptive** — its job is to set a shared vision and prompt focused action, not to dictate a fixed method. ODASD(SE) guides DoD Components in writing Service-level implementation plans that turn the vision into roadmaps and objectives.
- **DoD definition of digital engineering** — an integrated digital approach using authoritative sources of system data and models as a continuum across disciplines to support lifecycle activities from concept through disposal.
  - When/how: stated in the Vision (Section III) as the anchoring definition for the whole strategy.
- **The five Digital Engineering Goals** — introduced in the Goal Summary (Section IV) as the structure of the entire strategy (each goal is developed in later chapters):
  1. **Formalize the development, integration, and use of models** — making modeling deliberate practice that informs decisions at both the enterprise and the program level.
  2. **Provide an enduring, authoritative source of truth** — moving the primary means of communication from documents to digital models and data.
  3. **Incorporate technological innovation** to improve the engineering practice, extending beyond traditional model-based methods.
  4. **Establish a supporting infrastructure and environments** so stakeholders can perform activities, collaborate, and communicate.
  5. **Transform the culture and workforce** — equipping the Department to adopt digital engineering, and to sustain it, across the full lifecycle.
  - When/how: presented as a set (Figure 3), each goal later expanded into focus areas in Section V.
- **The methodology shift: design-build-test → model-analyze-build** — named in the Introduction as the paradigm change digital engineering enables, letting programs prototype, experiment, and test decisions in a virtual environment before fielding to the warfighter.
  - When/how: framed as the practical consequence of greater computing speed, storage, and processing capacity.

## Key Concepts
- **Why now**: traditional DoD acquisition has leaned on a linear, document-intensive, stove-piped process. The result is long cycle times and systems that are hard to change and sustain — a poor fit for rapidly shifting operational and threat environments and for exponential technology growth.
- **Authoritative source of truth (AST)**: rather than scattered documents, a common set of digital models and data becomes the primary means of communication, giving authorized stakeholders current, consistent, authoritative information across the lifecycle.
- **End-to-end digital enterprise**: the goal is to securely and safely connect people, processes, data, and capabilities so that models can digitally represent the **system of interest** — which the Strategy scopes broadly to include systems of systems, systems, processes, equipment, products, and parts — in the virtual world.
- **Continuum of models across the lifecycle**: using models is not new; what digital engineering emphasizes is *continuity* — the same authoritative models carried and reused from concept through disposal, rather than disposable, phase-local artifacts.
- **Enabling technologies**: the Strategy expects to fold in advanced computing, big data analytics, artificial intelligence, autonomous systems, and robotics to improve engineering practice.
- **Scope beyond engineering**: the transformation touches the research, requirements, acquisition, test, cost, sustainment, and intelligence communities, plus business operations such as acquisition practice, legal requirements, and contracted activities.
- **Expected benefits**: better-informed decisions, improved communication, greater understanding of and confidence in the design, and a more efficient engineering process (Figure 2).
- **Ownership and rollout**: ODASD(SE) leads and coordinates the actions; DoD Components and the Services produce implementation plans; the Defense Industrial Base and partners inside and outside the DoD stay engaged to keep alignment.

## Mental Models
- **Strategy as a compass, not a checklist**: the document deliberately avoids prescription. It supplies direction and a shared vision; the concrete "how" lives in Component and Service implementation plans. Read it as intent-setting, not procedure.
- **Models become the product of record, documents become a view**: the center of gravity moves from documents to a connected model-and-data backbone. Documents may still be generated, but they are renderings of the authoritative models, not the source.
- **Virtual-first engineering**: under model-analyze-build, you analyze and prove decisions in a virtual environment before committing to physical build and field — pushing discovery and risk-burndown earlier and cheaper.
- **One continuum, many disciplines**: the value comes from continuity. A model that stops at a phase boundary or a discipline boundary breaks the continuum and forfeits much of the benefit.
- **Transformation is socio-technical**: methods and tools are only part of it; goal 5 puts culture and workforce change on equal footing, because the shift fails if people and organizations do not adopt it.

## Key Takeaways
1. **Digital engineering** = an integrated, model-based approach using authoritative data and models as a continuum across disciplines and the full lifecycle (concept → disposal).
2. The driver is mission urgency: deliver capability faster amid exponential technology change, complexity, tight budgets, and aggressive schedules — problems the legacy linear, document-heavy process handles poorly.
3. The **2018 Strategy** is a *living, non-prescriptive* document from **ODASD(SE)**; it sets vision and direction, with execution detailed in DoD Component and Service implementation plans.
4. The strategy is organized around **five goals**: formalize models, authoritative source of truth, incorporate innovation, supporting infrastructure/environments, and culture/workforce transformation.
5. Digital engineering enables a shift from **design-build-test to model-analyze-build**, moving experimentation and testing into a virtual environment before fielding.
6. The transformation reaches **beyond engineering** — into requirements, acquisition, test, cost, sustainment, intelligence, and business/legal/contracting functions.
