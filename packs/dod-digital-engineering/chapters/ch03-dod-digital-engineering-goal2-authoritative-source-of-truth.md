# Chapter 3: Goal 2 — Provide an Enduring, Authoritative Source of Truth

## Core Idea
Goal 2 makes the models and data created under Goal 1 usable across the enterprise by establishing a single, trusted reference that any authorized stakeholder can reach to access, manage, protect, and analyze them. The Strategy frames this as a deliberate shift away from static, disconnected artifacts toward models and data that themselves become the connective tissue linking traditionally siloed elements, enabling an integrated exchange of information across the whole lifecycle. The plainest statement of intent in this section: deliver the right data, to the right person, for the right use, at the right time. Maintaining this authoritative reference well reduces the risk of acting on inaccurate model data and supports disciplined control of both current and historical configuration files.

## Frameworks Introduced
- **Authoritative source of truth (AST)** — defined in Focus Area 2.1 as the central reference point for models and data across the lifecycle. It captures both the current state and the history of the technical baseline.
  - When/how: positioned as the mechanism that provides traceability as the system of interest evolves, preserves historical knowledge, and connects the authoritative versions of models and data. Changes made to it propagate through the digital design model to every affected system and function.
- **Three focus areas of Goal 2** — the Strategy decomposes the goal into a plan/govern/use progression:
  - **2.1 Define the authoritative source of truth** — establish what it is and what it captures (current state + technical-baseline history) before anything else.
  - **2.2 Govern the authoritative source of truth** — put policies, procedures, access, and controls in place so the data stays formally managed and trusted.
  - **2.3 Use the authoritative source of truth across the lifecycle** — exercise it to develop, manage, and communicate system information from concept through disposal.
  - When/how: presented as the numbered focus areas under Goal 2 in Section V, each developed with its own set of activities.
- **Figure 5: Authoritative Source of Truth** — the Strategy's illustration of stakeholders collaboratively working within and through the AST using shared knowledge and resources across the lifecycle.
  - When/how: referenced in the Goal 2 overview to depict the collaborative, shared-resource model the goal enables.
- **U.S. Army LPDM/ePDM** — Lifecycle Product Data Management and enterprise Product Data Management, cited as a Service example of Goal 2 in practice.
  - When/how: presented as a sidebar example. The Army is applying a lifecycle approach to the large and complex body of product data needed across engineering design, acquisition, and sustainment of military systems. The LPDM/ePDM system gives the Army an authoritative source of truth for managing all weapon-system and end-item data throughout the lifecycle.

## Key Concepts
- **From documents to models as the basis for communication**: under Goal 2 the primary means of communication moves away from static, disconnected artifacts; models and data become what connects previously siloed elements and supports an integrated information exchange across the lifecycle.
- **Define before you build (2.1)**: realizing the AST depends on the up-front planning and model work of Goal 1. The Strategy stresses setting clear expectations for defining, developing, and using the AST across disciplines and throughout the lifecycle. Planning includes working out the data needs for acquisition and for engineering decisions, and integrating authoritative data sources seamlessly.
- **A structure for integrating disparate models and data**: the AST facilitates sharing across the boundaries of engineering disciplines, distributed teams, and other functional areas, and supplies the structure for organizing and integrating otherwise disparate models and data. It also provides the technical elements for creating, updating, retrieving, and integrating that content.
- **Governance keeps the data trusted (2.2)**: organizations establish policies and procedures so models and data are formally managed and trusted throughout the lifecycle, and so stakeholders collect, share, and maintain them accurately. Standardized procedures protect the integrity and quality of the data and keep it compliant with organizational and business rules. Governance also gives stakeholders a way to resolve issues and to keep the source consistent and accurate so decisions can be data-driven.
- **Access and controls (2.2)**: defining access and controls ensures authorized users get the right information at the right time and allows an uninterrupted flow of models and data across organizational boundaries. The balance the Strategy names is dual: data must be readily available to all intended recipients yet protected from unauthorized users, so information is appropriately created, managed, protected, and retained.
- **Governance is multi-level**: an effective governance process spans responsibilities at several levels; managing policies, procedures, and standards is what raises data quality and, in turn, stakeholder confidence in the integrity of the source.
- **Use across the lifecycle (2.3)**: the AST is used to develop, manage, and communicate system information from concept through disposal, and serves as the primary means to share and exchange models, data, and digital artifacts — equipping programs with enterprise-wide knowledge to plan, design, and sustain systems.
- **The AST as the technical baseline**: stakeholders use it to make informed, timely decisions about cost, schedule, performance, and risk. The worked example given is contract deliverables, which should be traced and validated from the AST — letting stakeholders at various levels respond knowledgeably to development, operation, and execution, and avoid technical and management barriers to mission success.
- **Producing digital artifacts**: as the technical baseline matures, preserving knowledge across programs and lifecycle phases matters. Technical reviews can be run from the AST on a continuous basis, and stakeholders generate digital artifacts representing multiple views and perspectives. Those artifacts give visibility into the right information across functional domains, disciplines, and organizations.
- **Collaboration and the document-to-model acceptance shift**: with access to up-to-date models and data, teams work collaboratively and integrate their work seamlessly. Building on Goal 1's continuum of models, this changes practice from accepting documents to accepting models, and underpins the acquisition domains and functional areas. Users generate various views from a shared network of models and data, cutting time-consuming effort and rework, so they can propose alternatives, collaborate across teams, promote reuse, raise productivity, and analyze the impact of change.
- **Functional reach of the AST**: the Strategy lists the functional areas served, spanning auditing, business cost estimating, business financial management, contracting, engineering, facilities engineering, industrial contract property management, information technology, lifecycle logistics, production/quality/manufacturing, program management, purchasing, science and technology management, and test and evaluation — illustrating that the source of truth is meant for the whole acquisition enterprise, not engineering alone.

## Mental Models
- **One reference point, not many copies**: the AST is the single central reference for models and data. Its value is that one authoritative version exists and that changes to it propagate to everything downstream — so the question for any artifact becomes "is this traced from the source of truth?" rather than "which copy is current?"
- **Right data, right person, right use, right time**: read the whole goal through this four-part test. Define (2.1) is what the data is; govern (2.2) is who may touch it and how it stays trustworthy; use (2.3) is the right use at the right time. Any AST decision can be checked against whether it serves all four.
- **Plan upstream or pay downstream**: the AST is realized through Goal 1's up-front planning. Skipping the early work of defining data needs and integrating sources means the source of truth never becomes authoritative — it just becomes another silo.
- **Governance buys trust, trust buys decisions**: the chain the Strategy draws is explicit — governance raises data quality, data quality raises stakeholder confidence in integrity, and that confidence is what lets people make data-driven decisions from the source. Without governance the AST is data nobody can rely on.
- **Open but guarded**: access control is framed as a deliberate tension to hold, not a one-sided lockdown. Maximize uninterrupted flow to intended recipients while keeping unauthorized users out — both halves are failure modes if dropped.
- **Accept the model, not the document**: the cultural pivot of Goal 2 is what stakeholders are willing to accept as the deliverable. Moving from accepting documents to accepting models is what makes the shared model-and-data network the real medium of exchange; documents become generated views.
- **Continuous reviews from a living baseline**: because the AST holds the maturing technical baseline, technical reviews and artifacts are drawn from it continuously rather than assembled as one-off packages at gate dates.

## Key Takeaways
1. **Goal 2 turns Goal 1's models into a shared, trusted reference** — the authoritative source of truth — so authorized stakeholders can access, manage, protect, and analyze them across the lifecycle.
2. The **AST is the central reference point** that captures the current state and history of the technical baseline, provides traceability as the system evolves, and propagates changes through the digital design model to all affected systems and functions.
3. The goal is structured as **define (2.1), govern (2.2), and use (2.3)** — establish what the AST is, keep it trusted through policy and access control, and exercise it from concept through disposal.
4. **Governance and access controls are the trust mechanism**: standardized procedures preserve integrity and compliance, while access controls keep data flowing to intended recipients yet protected from unauthorized users.
5. The AST becomes the **technical baseline for decisions** on cost, schedule, performance, and risk, with contract deliverables traced and validated from it, and supports continuous technical reviews and the generation of multi-view digital artifacts.
6. Goal 2 drives a **document-to-model acceptance shift** and serves the whole acquisition enterprise — a broad set of functional areas from contracting and logistics to test and evaluation — illustrated by the Army's LPDM/ePDM implementation.
7. The guiding aim throughout is to deliver **the right data, to the right person, for the right use, at the right time.**
