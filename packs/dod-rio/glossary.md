# DoD RIO Management Guide — Glossary

Key terms from the DoD Risk, Issue, and Opportunity (RIO) Management Guide, alphabetical,
with the chapter where each is treated. Definitions are synthesized from the guide; acronym
expansions are the source's own.

| Term | Meaning (as used in the guide) | Chapter |
|---|---|---|
| **AAF (Adaptive Acquisition Framework)** | The umbrella of six acquisition pathways (UCA, MTA, MCA, Software, DBS, Services) defined by DoDI 5000.02; the PM selects and tailors one. | ch01, ch05, ch06 |
| **Acquisition Strategy (AS)** | The top program-planning document; structures the program to mitigate key risks and records top risks with mitigations (DoDI 5000.85 for MCA). | ch02, ch04, ch05 |
| **Active Risk Manager** | Commercial RIO tool the Air Force directed for use. | ch08 |
| **Aggregate risk** | Overall program exposure from many risks interacting/compounding, beyond any single risk; modeled with Monte Carlo SRA/CRA. | ch02 |
| **Assess Only** | The lighter RMF path for sub-system technologies that do not need an Authority to Operate. | ch07 |
| **ATP (Authorization to Proceed)** | The DBS pathway's gate sequence (Solution Analysis, Functional Requirements, Acquisition, Limited Deployment, Full Deployment). | ch06 |
| **Burn-down chart / plan** | The six-step method and chart that plot a controlled risk's planned vs. actual likelihood/consequence descent over time (Fig. 2-8). | ch03, ch08 |
| **Business (External) risk** | Non-technical risk originating outside the program office or beyond the PM's control (dependencies, resources, regulations, market, weather). | ch02 |
| **Consequence** | The severity of a fully realized risk against cost, schedule, or performance baselines; scored 1–5, taking the *maximum* of the three, unmitigated. | ch02 |
| **Contingency plan** | A held mitigation activity that fires on a defined triggering event; recommended for high risks. | ch03 |
| **CRA (Cost Risk Analysis)** | Monte Carlo simulation of cumulative cost exposure; outputs histograms and S-curves. | ch02, ch08 |
| **CTT (Cyber Table Top)** | A wargame-style type of MBCRA pitting a mission force against a cyber force to surface mission impact of cyber attacks before operational test. | ch07 |
| **DCMA 14-point metrics** | The schedule structural-integrity check (logic, leads, lags, hard constraints, negative float, CPLI, BEI, etc.) gating SRA validity. | ch08 |
| **DTRAM (Defense Technical Risk Assessment Methodology)** | The ITRA method: eight risk areas assessed against seven factors. | ch07 |
| **EMV (Expected Monetary Value)** | Likelihood × cost-consequence-if-realized; minus mitigation cost gives an ROI used to rank competing risks (Table 2-3). | ch02 |
| **EVM (Earned Value Management)** | Cost/schedule performance baseline (EIA-748) into which mitigation work packages are integrated; EAC cross-checked against risk analysis. | ch03, ch08 |
| **Fit-gap analysis** | DBS step measuring the gap between a COTS/GOTS product's capabilities and the re-engineered business process, driving design specs and trades. | ch06 |
| **FMECA** | Failure Modes, Effects, and Criticality Analysis; required for all programs by DoDI 5000.88, built on the digital system representation. | ch07 |
| **Framing assumption** | An assumption (often unconscious) behind the Acquisition Strategy; an incorrect one can risk the whole program, so each is surfaced and validated. | ch05 |
| **IMS (Integrated Master Schedule)** | The baseline schedule where mitigation activities are placed and tracked; its health gates SRA. | ch03, ch08 |
| **Issue** | A negative event/condition that has already occurred or is essentially certain (likelihood = 5 / probability = 1); scored on consequence only. | ch01, ch03 |
| **ITRA (Independent Technical Risk Assessment)** | Congressionally required independent technical-risk view on MDAPs before milestone/production decisions (DoDI 5000.88). | ch05, ch07 |
| **JRMB (Joint Risk Management Board)** | A board for common Government–contractor risks, often co-chaired by both PMs. | ch08 |
| **Likelihood** | The evaluated probability an event occurs under existing conditions; scored 1–5 against probability bands (Table 2-2). | ch02 |
| **MBCRA (Mission-Based Cyber Risk Assessment)** | Analysis required by DoDI 5000.89 that prioritizes cyber risk by mission effect. | ch06, ch07 |
| **MCA (Major Capability Acquisition)** | The traditional milestone-driven hardware-centric pathway (MDD → MS A/B/C → IOC/FOC); DoDI 5000.85. | ch05 |
| **MOA / MOU** | Memorandum of Agreement/Understanding binding interdependent programs with cost/schedule/performance objectives and tripwires. | ch03, ch04 |
| **Mitigation options (four)** | Accept, Avoid, Transfer, Control — the menu for handling a risk (or a hybrid). | ch03 |
| **MTA (Middle Tier of Acquisition)** | The ~2–5-year pathway (Rapid Prototyping / Rapid Fielding); DoDI 5000.80; generally outside JCIDS by statute. | ch05 |
| **MVP / MVCR** | Minimum Viable Product / Minimum Viable Capability Release — the software pathway's risk-reducing iterative-delivery milestones. | ch06 |
| **Opportunity** | A potential future improvement to cost, schedule, or performance; the upside counterpart of risk; drives toward should-cost. | ch01, ch04 |
| **Opportunity options (four)** | Pursue, Defer, Reevaluate, Reject — the management decision for an approved opportunity. | ch04 |
| **PRA (Performance Risk Analysis)** | Engineering-discipline-specific risk analysis (e.g., probability of kill, ballistic testing). | ch08 |
| **PRMP (Program Risk Management Process / Plan)** | The program's foundational RIO planning document (non-mandatory label); records boards, criteria, tools, training, and the tailored five-step process. | ch01, ch02, ch08 |
| **Project Recon** | Army-developed RIO tool available DoD-wide. | ch08 |
| **RI3** | Risk Identification: Integration and Ilities (2008); an example identification methodology pairing key topics with trigger questions. | ch02 |
| **Risk** | A possible future event/condition that could harm cost, schedule, or performance; characterized by likelihood and consequence. | ch01, ch02 |
| **Risk Exchange** | Navy-developed RIO tool (Naval Systems Engineering Resource Center). | ch08 |
| **Risk register** | The central repository tracking each risk's category, statement, scores, mitigation, owner, WBS/IMS linkage, and history; may combine risk/issue/opportunity. | ch02, ch08 |
| **Risk Reporting Matrix** | The 5×5 likelihood × max-consequence grid mapping each risk to low (green)/moderate (yellow)/high (red) (Fig. 2-5/2-6). | ch02 |
| **Risk statement** | Structured event + consequence (+ cause if known), often "if–then"; excludes any mitigation. | ch02 |
| **RMB (Risk Management Board)** | The PM-chaired senior body that adjudicates risks (and usually opportunities), assigns owners, and approves mitigation/resourcing. | ch02, ch03, ch08 |
| **RMF (Risk Management Framework)** | The cybersecurity risk approach (DoDI 8510.01); seven steps Prepare→Categorize→Select→Implement→Assess→Authorize→Monitor; tiered per NIST SP 800-39. | ch07 |
| **ROMB** | Risk and Opportunity Management Board — an RMB that also handles opportunities. | ch04, ch08 |
| **RWG (Risk Working Group)** | A chartered working-level group led from the SE or PM IPT, feeding the RMB. | ch03, ch08 |
| **SEP (Systems Engineering Plan)** | Summarizes the risk process and records technical risks/mitigation execution (DoDI 5000.88); may refer out to the PRMP. | ch02, ch05, ch08 |
| **Should-cost vs. will-cost** | Will-cost = the estimate the program stays inside; should-cost = the lower target opportunity management actively drives toward (Fig. 3-1). | ch04 |
| **SRA (Schedule Risk Analysis)** | Monte Carlo simulation of cumulative schedule exposure; an indicator, not a verdict; gated by IMS health. | ch02, ch08 |
| **SWAP-C** | Size, Weight, Power, and Cooling — an allocation interdependent programs must reconcile across interfaces. | ch04, ch05 |
| **Technical risk** | Risk that the end item won't perform as intended; splits into Technology, Engineering, and Integration sub-types. | ch02 |
| **TPM (Technical Performance Measure)** | Metric (required by DoDI 5000.02, kept SMART) tracking technical progress and tied to risk burn-down. | ch08 |
| **Tripwire** | A cost/schedule/performance threshold in a cross-program MOA that forces notification of family-of-systems partners on a significant variance. | ch04 |
| **UCA (Urgent Capability Acquisition)** | The <2-year pathway for urgent operational needs (DoDD 5000.71 / DoDI 5000.81); can carry accepted deficiencies; ends in disposition analysis. | ch05 |
| **User Agreement (UA)** | The software pathway's risk-bearing device committing operational-user resourcing and capability-prioritization decision authority. | ch06 |
| **Watch Item** | An accepted risk that is still actively tracked (acceptance ≠ ignoring), with re-evaluation knowledge points. | ch03 |
| **WBS (Work Breakdown Structure)** | The task checklist (MIL-STD-881F) ensuring every task is examined for risk; mitigation links to its work packages. | ch08 |
